## Key Findings and Surprises

- **The mTOR pathway convergence is remarkable**: Two independent GWAS loci (DEPTOR at 8q24.12, NPRL3 at 16p13.3) both encode negative regulators of mTOR. This is among the strongest pathway-level genetic signals in IPF, yet NO trial directly targets mTOR in IPF.
- **IL-7 is a "hidden gem"**: MR shows causal protective effect (OR=0.67) with coloc support (PP.H4=0.84). Recombinant IL-7 already exists (CYT107). This could be tested rapidly.
- **GPR157 is a textbook orphan GPCR target**: Genome-wide significant in the largest GWAS, protective direction, and GPCRs are the most druggable protein class. De-orphanization effort warranted.
- **The spindle assembly cluster (KIF15, KNL1, MAD1L1, STMN3) remains unexplained**: Four GWAS genes in the same pathway is striking but biologically puzzling for a fibrotic disease. Possible link to alveolar epithelial regeneration defects.
- **DPP9 is actionable NOW**: GWAS p=1.7e-12, colocalized in IPF lung tissue, and DPP4 family inhibitors (sitagliptin) are approved drugs. Molecular docking confirms binding. This could be a near-term clinical hypothesis.

## Limitations and Uncertainties

1. Many TWAS and MR effect sizes could not be fully extracted (publications reported summary findings without complete tables)
2. DepMap scores were assessed from literature, not directly queried — individual gene scores needed
3. Some druggability assessments are approximate based on protein class rather than individual compound data
4. The 17q21 MAPT/KANSL1 locus remains complex due to the inversion polymorphism
5. European ancestry bias persists across most evidence layers
6. No original statistical computation was performed — all evidence curated from publications
