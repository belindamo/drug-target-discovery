### 1. DPP9-NLRP1 Inflammasome Comprehensive Deep-Dive

Resolved the DPP9 therapeutic paradox with structural-level detail:

**Structural biology:**
- Cryo-EM of human NLRP1-DPP9 at 3.6 Å (PDB 6X6A/6X6C; Hollingsworth Nature 2021 PMID:33731932) and rat complex (PDB 7CRW; Huang Nature 2021 PMID:33731929)
- 2:1 NLRP1:DPP9 ternary complex: ZU5 domain of full-length NLRP1 binds DPP9 (interface I, ~1000 Å²); freed CT fragment threads UPA into DPP9 substrate tunnel (interface II, ~1600 Å²)
- DPP9 acts as "bomb defuser" — sequesters freed NLRP1-CT fragments preventing inflammasome assembly
- Both catalytic and scaffolding functions required for NLRP1 repression (Zhong JBC 2018 PMID:30291141)

**Paradox resolution:**
- DPP9 inhibition activates NLRP1 → pyroptosis → IL-1β/IL-18 → worsens fibrosis
- Dpp9-S759A homozygous mice die within 24h; rescued by single-copy deletion of Nlrp1/Asc/Gsdmd/Il-1r (Harapas SciImmunol 2022 PMID:36112693)
- Specifically IL-1R (not IL-18) rescue confirms IL-1β as the critical fibrotic effector
- No small-molecule DPP9 activators exist; pinocembrin (flavonoid) upregulates DPP9 transcriptionally but not direct activation

**Downstream therapeutic alternatives ranked:**
1. **Anakinra/canakinumab** (IL-1β blockade) — approved, safe, never tested in IPF
2. **ADS032** (dual NLRP1/NLRP3 inhibitor; Adiso; CTI 2023 PMID:37360982) — advanced preclinical
3. **Disulfiram** (GSDMD inhibitor; FDA-approved; NatImmunol PMID:32367036) — repurposable
4. **Dimethyl fumarate** (GSDMD + NRF2; FDA-approved for MS) — combination strategy

**Updated colocalization:** Dalapati 2025 published PP4=0.968 in fibroblasts (HGG Advances PMID:39876559), correcting preprint value of 0.989. Also: PP4=0.874 in IPF cases, 0.661 in controls (disease-specific). DPP9 decreased in AT2 cells + monocytes but INCREASED in myofibroblasts (scRNA-seq).

### 2. MUC1 Target Upgrade with Comprehensive Functional Evidence

Integrated Chin 2025 GWAS data with Milara 2020 functional validation:

**GWAS specifics (now with exact numbers):**
- rs9426886: OR=1.162 (95% CI 1.106-1.219), p=1.53×10⁻⁹, EAF=0.568
- Replicated GBMI p=8.02×10⁻³
- 7-SNP credible set, PIP=0.208 for lead variant
- Pleiotropic with urate (p=1.5×10⁻¹⁰¹) and haematocrit (p=4.7×10⁻⁴¹)

**Functional evidence chain:**
- TGFβ1 → Smad3 → MUC1-CT phosphorylation (Thr41/Tyr46) → pSmad3/MUC1-CT/β-catenin nuclear complex → fibrotic gene activation
- MUC1-KO mice protected from bleomycin fibrosis
- GO-201 peptide inhibitor reduces fibrosis in vivo
- Galectin-3 activates MUC1-CT independently of TGFβ1
- Pirfenidone partially works via MUC1-CT phosphorylation inhibition (PMID:32341751)

**Clinical biomarker integration:**
- KL-6 ≥1000 U/mL predicts progression (HR=2.761, p=0.040)
- KL-6 ≥1200 U/mL predicts death in IPF+OSA (HR=4.887, p=0.039)
- Theranostic potential: KL-6 for patient selection + MUC1-CT inhibitor for therapy

**Druggability:**
- DS-3939 ADC: ESMO 2025 Phase 1/2, 11/64 responses (cancer; TA-MUC1 glycoepitope not IPF-relevant)
- GO-203 (MUC1-C dimerization inhibitor) in Phase I/II for AML (NCT02204085)
- Antibody modality: HIGH tractability (membrane protein, large extracellular domain)
- Small molecule: LOW (no conventional binding pocket)

### 3. NTN4 Biology — Complete Receptor and Functional Profile

**Key paradigm-shifting finding: NTN4 does NOT bind classical netrin-1 receptors.**
- No DCC, UNC5A-D binding (crystal structure at 3.1 Å; NatComm 2016 PMID:27901020)
- Binds neogenin directly (PNAS 2008 PMID:18719102)
- UNC5B recruited indirectly via neogenin-UNC5B complex
- Primary molecular target: laminin gamma-1 chain (1:1, Kd nM; Schneiders JBC 2007 PMID:17588941)
- NTN4 is 50% identical to laminin beta-1 (structural outlier in netrin family)

**IPF-relevant biology:**
- Expressed in aberrant basaloid cells — IPF-specific cell population at myofibroblast foci edges (Adams SciAdv 2020 PMID:32832599)
- Anti-senescence: reduces SA-βgal, CDKN1A, CDKN2A in ECs (PMID:33636396)
- Anti-inflammatory: NTN4 silencing increases ICAM-1/VCAM-1, monocyte adhesion
- Regulates lung branching morphogenesis (Dev Cell 2004 PMID:15186747)
- Inhibits Wnt/β-catenin in ccRCC via GSK3β (Cancers 2023 PMC10216638)

**Distinction from NTN1 (critical for IPF interpretation):**
- NTN1 (macrophage-derived) is pro-fibrotic in lung (JCI 2021 PMID:33393489) — drives adrenergic nerve-associated fibrosis
- NTN1 (epithelial-derived) is protective (ATS 2025) — cell-type dependent
- NTN1 also pro-fibrotic in liver (Advanced Science 2025/2026, HSC-derived)
- NTN4 has completely different receptor biology — cannot extrapolate NTN1 findings to NTN4

**GWAS data (now with exact numbers):**
- rs7957346: OR=1.153 (95% CI 1.098-1.212), p=1.56×10⁻⁸; replicated p=4.69×10⁻⁵
- In 3'-UTR of SNRPF; 5-SNP credible set, PIP=0.376
- Risk allele also associated with decreased lung function (p=7.8×10⁻⁴⁵)
- NTN4 has highest coloc at locus (PP.H4=0.649) but below 0.8 threshold

### 4. Pipeline Failures Pattern Analysis

Documented three major IPF trial failures in 2024-2025, ALL lacking genetic support:

| Drug | Target | Failure Mode |
|---|---|---|
| Bexotegrast | αvβ6/αvβ1 integrins | Safety: DSMB stop (worsening IPF events) |
| GB0139/Olitigaltin | Galectin-3 (inhaled) | Efficacy: no target engagement; FVC WORSE than placebo |
| Zinpentraxin alfa (PRM-151) | Pentraxin-2 | Futility: Roche $400M write-off |

Counter-examples: Nerandomilast (PDE4B; approved) and treprostinil (prostacyclin; TETON-2 positive) succeeded without genetic support.

**Overall failure rate without genetics: 3/5 = 60%** — consistent with the 2-3x enrichment for success with genetically supported targets (King et al., Nat Genet 2019).

## Key Findings and Surprises

1. **DPP9-NLRP1 structural data is remarkably clear** — the 2:1 ternary complex explains the dose-sensitivity (even partial DPP9 loss shifts equilibrium toward inflammasome activation)
2. **Dalapati 2025 published PP4=0.968 (not 0.989)** — the fibroblast colocalization remains strong but corrected downward from preprint
3. **NTN4 receptor biology is completely different from NTN1** — this invalidates any extrapolation of NTN1 pro-fibrotic findings to NTN4; the netrin family is NOT functionally interchangeable
4. **MUC1 has more functional IPF evidence than most Tier 1 targets** — KO protection + in vivo pharmacology + pirfenidone mechanism + biomarker — but the GWAS coloc (0.616) is the weakest link
5. **Three high-profile failures without genetic support** form a pattern: genetic evidence may not be sufficient for success, but lack of it correlates with higher failure rates in IPF specifically

## Limitations

1. Dalapati 2025 correction (0.968 vs 0.989) is modest but demonstrates the importance of using published (not preprint) values
2. NTN4 direction of effect still unresolved — whether risk allele increases or decreases NTN4 expression determines augment vs inhibit strategy
3. MUC1 coloc PP=0.616 remains below threshold; fine-mapping PIP=0.208 means the causal variant is uncertain
4. Pipeline failure analysis has small sample size (n=5); pattern is suggestive not definitive
5. All evidence remains literature-extracted, not de novo computation
