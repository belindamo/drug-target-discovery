### 1. DPP9 Pharmacological Landscape: Three Critical New Findings

Resolved outstanding pharmacology questions for DPP9, the #2-ranked IPF target, with three transformative findings that fundamentally reshape the therapeutic strategy.

#### 1a. Compound 42 Has Fatal PK Limitation; ICeD-2 Is the Better Tool

From the Nguyen et al. 2025 comprehensive review in *Cellular and Molecular Life Sciences* (PMID pending; DOI: 10.1007/s00018-025-05719-4):

| Compound | DPP9 Ki/IC₅₀ | DPP9/DPP8 Selectivity | Oral Bioavailability | Half-life | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Compound 42 (Benramdane 2023) | 3 nM | 175x | **2%** | **<45 min** | Tool compound only |
| ICeD-2 (Moore 2022, PMID:36044633) | ~1 nM | 27x | **61%** | **20 h** | Best in vivo tool |
| 1G244 | ~nM | <5x (pan-DPP8/9) | Limited data | - | Legacy tool |
| Val-boroPro (VbP) | ~nM | Non-selective | In vivo active | - | Pan-DPP inhibitor |

**Key insight**: Compound 42, previously highlighted as the most selective DPP9 inhibitor (175x), has only **2% oral bioavailability and <45 min half-life** — ruling it out for in vivo applications without formulation optimization. **ICeD-2** (Merck; ACS Chem Biol 2022, PMID:36044633), with 27x selectivity, 61% bioavailability, and 20h half-life, is currently the **best DPP9 tool compound for in vivo studies**. The F253 residue identified by Benramdane enables future selectivity engineering.

#### 1b. TC-E5007 Is ANTI-FIBROTIC in Kidney — Challenging the Simple Paradox

Critical finding from Ren et al. 2021 (Pharmacological Research, PMID:33932609):

- DPP8 and DPP9 expression is **increased** in CKD patient tubulointerstitium (Nephroseq database) and correlates with declining kidney function
- TC-E5007 (dual DPP8/DPP9 inhibitor) **significantly attenuates EMT and ECM synthesis** in the UUO (unilateral ureteral obstruction) mouse model of renal fibrosis
- Mechanism: TC-E5007 inhibits TGF-β1/Smad signaling pathway
- siRNA knockdown of DPP8 and DPP9 independently recapitulates the anti-fibrotic effect
- Follow-up study (PMID:38458339): DPP8/9 inhibition also reduces ECM in glomerular mesangial cells via Smad and Akt pathways
- 2025 study: TC-E5007 protects from tubular epithelial cell ferroptosis in acute kidney injury

**This is paradigm-shifting**: DPP8/9 inhibition is **anti-fibrotic** in kidney, operating through TGF-β1/Smad — completely independent of the NLRP1 inflammasome mechanism. This suggests DPP8/9 have cell-type-specific and context-dependent profibrotic roles that are SEPARATE from their inflammasome-suppressive role.

**Updated DPP9 Therapeutic Model (Session 6)**:

```
DPP9 has THREE mechanistic axes:

  Axis 1: NLRP1/CARD8 inflammasome suppression (immune cells)
    → Inhibition ACTIVATES inflammasome → pro-inflammatory → BAD for IPF
    → Genetics: ↓DPP9 = ↑risk (via this axis)

  Axis 2: TGF-β1/Smad-mediated EMT/ECM (epithelial cells, fibroblasts)
    → Inhibition BLOCKS fibrotic EMT → anti-fibrotic → GOOD for fibrosis
    → Evidence: TC-E5007 in UUO model (PMID:33932609)
    → Evidence: FAP↑ → DPP9↓ → EMT in OSCC (PMID:32273729)

  Axis 3: BRISC/PD-L1 immune regulation (cancer cells)
    → Not directly relevant to IPF but shows mechanistic breadth

  NET EFFECT of DPP9 inhibition in IPF:
    → Depends on cell type, dose, and tissue context
    → Low-dose fibroblast-targeted DPP9 inhibition could be anti-fibrotic
    → High-dose systemic DPP9 inhibition would trigger inflammasome
    → THERAPEUTIC WINDOW EXISTS but is narrow
```

#### 1c. Pinocembrin: First Identified DPP9 Activator

From Wang et al. 2024 (Immunologic Research, published Dec 2024):

- **Pinocembrin** (5,7-dihydroxyflavanone), a natural flavonoid from honey/propolis, **activates DPP9 expression**
- In MCAO/R (middle cerebral artery occlusion/reperfusion) mice: pinocembrin → ↑DPP9 → ↓NLRP1 inflammasome → protects brain, **lung**, and intestine
- DPP9 knockdown reverses pinocembrin's protective effects → confirms DPP9 is the target
- Pinocembrin is in Phase II clinical trials in China (CFDA-approved) for ischemic stroke

**Significance**: This is the first demonstration that DPP9 can be pharmacologically ACTIVATED — partially resolving the paradox. However, pinocembrin is a pleiotropic flavonoid (not a clean DPP9 activator), and its IPF relevance is indirect (stroke model, not fibrosis model).

#### 1d. Nguyen 2025 CMLS Review: Counter-Intuitive DPP9 Biology

The comprehensive Nguyen et al. 2025 review (Cell Mol Life Sci, DOI:10.1007/s00018-025-05719-4) reveals:

- DPP9 gene expression and enzyme activity **INCREASES** in colitis and asthma
- Treatment with DPP8/9-selective inhibitors **REDUCES** inflammation, eosinophilia, and swelling in asthma, colitis, and arthritis models
- This contradicts the NLRP1 paradigm (which predicts DPP9 inhibition = more inflammation)
- The review concludes: "DPP9 regulation of inflammatory response lacks clarity, including how much DPP9 is required to prevent a pro-inflammatory state"

**Implication for IPF**: The assumption that DPP9 inhibition = worse IPF is an oversimplification. In fibrotic/inflammatory contexts, DPP8/9 may be upregulated as part of the pathological response, and their inhibition may be therapeutic via non-inflammasome mechanisms.

### 2. MUC1 Upgraded from Tier 2 to Tier 1: Functional Driver, Not Just Biomarker

Comprehensive literature search revealed MUC1 (Chin 2025 GWAS locus, rs9426886) is far more than the KL-6 biomarker epitope. Key evidence:

**Milara et al. 2020, Thorax (PMID:31801904)**:
- MUC1-CT (cytoplasmic tail) phosphorylation at Thr41 and Tyr46 is **increased in IPF patient tissue** (n=22 IPF vs n=21 healthy)
- Localized to fibroblasts and hyperplastic alveolar type II cells
- TGFβ1 → Smad3 → phosphorylates MUC1-CT → activates β-catenin
- MUC1-CT/β-catenin/pSmad2/3 nuclear complex drives both **EMT** (epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition) and **FMT** (fibroblast-to-myofibroblast transition)
- **MUC1-KO mice are PROTECTED from bleomycin-induced lung fibrosis**
- **GO-201** (peptide inhibitor blocking MUC1-CT dimerization via CQC motif) reduces myofibroblast transition, senescence, and proliferation in vitro AND reduces fibrosis in vivo
- Galectin-3 bridges TGF-β receptor to MUC1-C domain, providing TGFβ-dependent AND TGFβ-independent activation

**Released KL-6/MUC1 ectodomain**:
- Cleaved by metalloproteinases (increased in IPF)
- Promotes fibroblast migration, proliferation, and myofibroblast transformation
- Established IPF progression biomarker (serum levels correlate with FVC decline)

**Therapeutic Agents**:
- **GO-201/GO-203**: Peptide inhibitors targeting MUC1-CT CQC motif; GO-203 (D-amino acid, more stable) in oncology clinical development
- **GO-203 nanoparticles**: Polymeric NP formulation published (PMC4433879) improving intracellular delivery
- **Anti-MUC1 antibodies and ADCs**: Multiple oncology programs targeting MUC1 ectodomain

**Ranking Implication**: MUC1 moved from rank 16 (Tier 2 biomarker) to **rank 8** (Tier 1 Strong, direction = inhibit CT domain). The combination of GWAS evidence + KO mouse protection + therapeutic peptide + distinct MUC1-CT mechanism (separate from MUC5B) makes this a compelling novel target.

### 3. NTN4 (Netrin-4): Characterized as Vascular/Angiogenic Pathway Target

Expanded on Session 5's NTN4 analysis:

- NTN4 is **26.6-fold overexpressed in lung** (GeneCards/GTEx), explaining the strong coloc signal
- Pharos classification: **Tbio** (biologically characterized but undrugged)
- Anti-angiogenic via neogenin + UNC5B co-receptor binding (PNAS 2008, PMID:18719102)
- Disrupts basement membrane via high-affinity laminin γ1 binding (Nat Commun 2016, PMID:27901020)
- Anti-senescence and anti-inflammatory in endothelial cells (PMID:33621610)
- **Netrin-1 family context**: NTN1 is PRO-FIBROTIC — macrophage-derived NTN1 drives adrenergic nerve-associated lung fibrosis (JCI 2021, PMID:33393489)
- NTN4 clusters with ACVRL1 in the vascular/angiogenic pathway relevant to IPF endothelial dysfunction

## Key Findings and Surprises

### 1. The DPP9 Paradox Is MORE Nuanced Than Session 5 Concluded

Session 5 resolved the paradox as: "DPP9 inhibition = bad → target IL-1β downstream instead." Session 6 reveals this was an oversimplification:

- **TC-E5007** (DPP8/9 inhibitor) is **anti-fibrotic** in kidney via TGF-β1/Smad blockade (PMID:33932609)
- **Nguyen 2025**: DPP8/9 inhibitors are paradoxically **anti-inflammatory** in asthma and colitis
- DPP9 has **tissue-specific, dose-dependent effects** — low-dose fibroblast-targeted inhibition may be anti-fibrotic while sparing immune cells

The updated model recognizes **three mechanistic axes** (inflammasome, EMT/TGF-β, immune regulation) with opposing effects of inhibition in different cell types. A narrow therapeutic window may exist for **partial, cell-type-specific DPP9 modulation**.

### 2. MUC1 Is the Biggest Ranking Mover This Session

MUC1 moved from rank 16 (Tier 2 biomarker) to **rank 8** (Tier 1 Strong driver). The key evidence:
- MUC1-KO mice protected from bleomycin fibrosis
- GO-201 peptide inhibitor anti-fibrotic in vivo
- Clear mechanistic pathway: MUC1-CT/β-catenin/Smad2/3 nuclear complex
- Distinct from MUC5B — independent mucin pathway
- Druggable via peptide (GO-203) or antibody modalities

### 3. Pinocembrin Is the First Identified DPP9 Activator

The discovery that pinocembrin activates DPP9 expression (Immunol Res 2024) partially resolves the pharmacological paradox. However, pinocembrin is a pleiotropic flavonoid, not a clean DPP9 activator. Development of selective small-molecule DPP9 activators (allosteric or transcriptional) should be a priority.

### 4. The Spindle Assembly Pathway Is Independently Validated by SPDL1

SPDL1 (CommBiol 2021) provides an independent genetic anchor for the spindle assembly pathway in IPF. Now with 4+ genes (KIF15, MAD1L1, KNL1, SPDL1, STMN3) and functional evidence (aberrant spindle morphology, chromosome segregation errors), this represents a bona fide non-telomeric mechanism of IPF.

### 5. Zelasudil Provides First Direct Genetic-Clinical Convergence

The AKAP13 → RhoA → ROCK → zelasudil (Phase 2a IPF) chain is the first instance in our pipeline where a GWAS-supported gene directly maps to a drug in an active IPF clinical trial via a clear mechanistic pathway. If zelasudil succeeds, it would validate the genetics-first approach.

## Limitations and Uncertainties

1. **TC-E5007 kidney fibrosis data may not translate to lung**: Kidney and lung fibrosis share TGF-β mechanisms but differ in cell composition, ECM architecture, and inflammatory milieu.

2. **Pinocembrin's DPP9 activation is indirect**: Mechanism of transcriptional upregulation unknown; pinocembrin has many other targets (anti-oxidant, anti-inflammatory, anti-apoptotic).

3. **MUC1 coloc PP = 0.616**: Below our standard 0.8 threshold. Upgrade to Tier 1 is based on functional evidence compensating for weaker statistical colocalization.

4. **ICeD-2 selectivity (27x) may be insufficient**: In vivo, 27x DPP9/DPP8 selectivity could lead to DPP8-mediated off-target effects; DPP8 has its own inflammasome biology.

5. **NTN4 direction paradox unresolved**: Session 5 flagged this; Session 6 confirms no new resolution available from literature.

6. **SLC6A6 remains uncharacterized in fibrosis**: Added to ranking but with minimal evidence; could be a false positive or a very early-stage discovery.

7. **Zelasudil Phase 2a is early**: Clinical hold was lifted March 2026; outcomes will take 12-18 months. ROCK inhibitors have pleiotropic effects beyond AKAP13 pathway.

## Conclusion

Session 6 significantly deepened the mechanistic understanding of the IPF target landscape. The most important finding is that DPP9 biology is far more nuanced than a simple "inflammasome suppressor" — it has three independent mechanistic axes with opposing therapeutic implications in different cell types. Critically, DPP8/9 inhibition is anti-fibrotic in kidney (PMID:33932609), suggesting a therapeutic window may exist for fibroblast-targeted partial DPP9 inhibition in lung.

MUC1's promotion to rank 8 (Tier 1) based on MUC1-KO mouse protection and GO-201 anti-fibrotic efficacy makes it the second novel mucin target (after MUC5B) with both genetic and functional validation. The pipeline now contains 25 ranked targets across 6 biological pathways, with clear convergence points that inform combination therapy strategies.

The identification of zelasudil (ROCK inhibitor, Phase 2a IPF) as the first drug in an active IPF trial with direct genetic rationale via AKAP13 validates the genetics-first approach. If zelasudil succeeds, it would be the first genetically-informed IPF therapeutic success.
