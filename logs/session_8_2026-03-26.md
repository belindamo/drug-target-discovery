### 2. DPP9-KEAP1-NRF2 Redox Axis — New Biology Integrated

Discovered and characterized the DPP9-KEAP1 mutual inhibition complex (Honan et al., JBC 2024, PMID: 39615677):
- Unfolded DPP9 binds KEAP1 via buried ESGE motif → prevents NRF2 degradation → activates antioxidant response
- DPP9 loss → reduced KEAP1 sequestration → NRF2 degradation → oxidative stress → fibrosis amplification
- Supporting evidence: DPP9 stabilizes NRF2 to suppress ferroptosis in ccRCC (Zhong et al., Cancer Res 2023, PMID: 37713596)
- 2025 fluorescent DPP8/9 sensor (DiPAK) confirms DPP8/9 fulfil functions via both substrate processing AND protein binding (KEAP1, NLRP1, CARD8)

**Significance:** This creates a double-hit model for DPP9 loss in IPF: simultaneous NLRP1 inflammasome activation AND oxidative stress via NRF2 loss. Suggests NRF2 activators (dimethyl fumarate, approved for MS) may partially compensate.

### 3. NTN4 (Netrin-4) — Comprehensive Biological Characterization

Performed extensive web search and literature synthesis for NTN4 (novel IPF GWAS locus, 12q23.1):

**Structural biology:**
- NTN4 homologous to laminin β1 chain (50% identity) — unique among netrins
- Binds laminin γ1 chain with high affinity; actively disrupts pre-existing laminin networks non-enzymatically (Reuter et al., Nature Comms 2016, PMID: 27901020)
- Not recognized by canonical netrin receptors (DCC, UNC5)
- Softens basement membrane stiffness by opening laminin node complexes (Reuten et al., Nature Materials 2021, PMID: 33495631)

**Anti-senescence/anti-inflammatory (Xu et al., BBRC 2021, PMID: 33652203):**
- NTN4 reduction → increased CDKN1A/CDKN2A (senescence markers) + VCAM-1/ICAM-1 (inflammation)
- NTN4 reduction → impaired endothelial barrier function
- Rescued by exogenous NTN4 protein

**26.6-fold enriched in lung (GeneCards)** — high tissue specificity for IPF relevance

**IPF mechanistic hypothesis:** NTN4 dysregulation → BM disruption → vascular instability → endothelial senescence → impaired alveolar-capillary repair. Represents a novel vascular/endothelial pathway distinct from epithelial/fibroblast paradigm.

### 4. IL-1β Translational Gap Confirmed via Systematic Search

Web search confirmed: **No clinical trial of anakinra or canakinumab in IPF exists as of March 2026**, despite:
- DPP9 GWAS → NLRP1/CARD8 inflammasome → IL-1β (genetically validated chain)
- DPP9 KO lethality rescued by single Il-1r allele deletion (PMID: 36112693)
- NLRP3/NLRP1/AIM2 inflammasomes confirmed active in IPF macrophages
- Inhaled anakinra effective in CF models (PMID: 36442616)
- IL-1β overexpression directly induces pulmonary fibrosis in transient expression models (PMID: 12907625)

**Current IPF pipeline lacks IL-1 targeting entirely:**
- Nerandomilast (PDE4B) — Phase 3 positive
- Rentosertib (pan-TRK) — Phase 2a positive
- Taladegib (Hedgehog) — Phase 2b
- Treprostinil (prostacyclin) — TETON trials
- None have GWAS support for their target

**NLRP3 inhibitors in clinical development (but not for IPF):**
- DFV890/IFM2427 (Phase I, inflammatory diseases + fibrosis)
- NT-0796 (NodThera, Phase Ib/IIa, neuroinflammation + cardiovascular)
- VENT-02 (Ventus, Phase 1, brain-penetrant)
- ADS032 (Adiso, dual NLRP1/NLRP3, preclinical lung models)

### 5. MUC1 GO-203 Drug Development Status Updated

Extended GO-203 characterization:
- GO-203 = all D-amino acid peptide with poly-R transduction domain + CQCRRKN motif → blocks MUC1-C homodimerization
- Phase I completed in solid tumors (NCT01279603)
- Phase I/II ongoing in AML
- Johns Hopkins 2024: GO-203 blocks post-hypoxic metastatic cell spread to lung
- Nanoparticle formulation (PLA-(PEG-PPG-PEG)) addresses pharmacokinetic limitations
- Induces ferroptosis via GSR/LRP8/GPX4 suppression
- 2025 review (npj Precision Oncology): MUC1 as "promising pan-cancer therapeutic target" with vaccines, CAR-T, ADCs, radiopharma
