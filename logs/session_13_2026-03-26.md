### 1. DPP9-NLRP1 Inflammasome Deep-Dive

Completed the most comprehensive structural and mechanistic analysis of DPP9 in the pipeline:

**Structural biology:**
- 2:1 ternary complex: one autoinhibited NLRP1 + one active UPA-CARD fragment per DPP9 dimer (Huang 2021 PMID:33731929; Hollingsworth 2021 PMID:33731932; PDB 6X6A/6X6C/7CRW)
- Both catalytic activity AND physical scaffolding required for NLRP1 suppression
- CARD8 uses a threshold-setting mechanism: DPP9 enzymatic activity (not binding) is primary (Sharif 2021 Immunity PMID:34019797)
- VbP displaces NLRP1 C-terminus from DPP9 active site → releases UPA-CARD → ASC → caspase-1 → pyroptosis

**Key in vivo evidence:**
- Dpp9 S759A/S759A mice die within 24h (Harapas 2022 Sci Immunol PMID:36112693)
- Rescue by single-allele deletion of Nlrp1, Asc, Gsdmd, or Il-1r (NOT Il-18)
- Confirms IL-1β as primary pathogenic effector
- Heterozygous Il-1r deletion sufficient — steep dose-response threshold supports partial anti-IL-1β therapy

**Novel findings integrated:**
- **Pinocembrin** is the first published DPP9 activator/inducer (Wang 2025 Immunol Res) — upregulates DPP9 → suppresses NLRP1 in lung injury
- **DPP9-KEAP1 complex** — mutually inhibitory; C844 critical (Tsamouri 2025 JBC PMID:39615677). DPP9 loss = double hit: ↑inflammasome + ↓antioxidant
- **Lung-specific DPP9-AFE isoform** (bioRxiv 2025) — alternative first exon with p.Leu8Pro coding variant from GWAS signal; explains lung specificity
- **Renal fibrosis context**: DPP8/9 are UPREGULATED in CKD and their INHIBITION is antifibrotic (Tang 2021 PMID:33932609; Jiang 2024 PMID:38458339) — opposite direction from IPF genetics
- **Covalent DPP8/9 inhibitors**: Sulphostin derivatives with improved selectivity (Sewald 2025 Nat Commun)
- **Comprehensive review**: Nguyen 2025 CMLS (PMID:40293537) — DPP9 dysregulated in fibrosis, tumor development, pyroptosis, inflammatory responses

### 2. MUC1 Comprehensive Functional Validation

Built the most complete MUC1-IPF evidence dossier:

**Biomarker performance (quantified):**
- KL-6 diagnostic AUC = 0.96 (highest among IPF serological biomarkers; PMID:31665737)
- Clinical cutoff 500 U/mL: sensitivity 85.3%, specificity 90.0%
- Prognostic: HR=2.05 mortality, HR=1.98 progression (pooled meta-analysis, Zhang 2021 PMID:34956179)
- ≥1000 U/mL threshold: median survival 18 vs >36 months (PMID:16548901)
- Acute exacerbation OR=2.72 (Bergantini 2021 PMID:33831978)
- Serial change: HR=1.10 per 100 U/mL increase (Sokai 2017 PMID:28875146)

**Cell-type-specific eQTL coloc (NEW — Natri 2024, PMID:38548990):**
- MUC1 eQTL colocalizes with IPF GWAS in **AT2, transitional AT2, and AT1 cells**
- Also colocalizes with UKBB IPF GWAS and EAS IPF GWAS in AT2 cells
- Directly implicates alveolar epithelial cells where MUC1-CT bioactivation drives fibrosis

**Mechanistic biology:**
- MUC1-CT bioactivation: TGF-β1 → pSMAD3 → phospho-MUC1-CT (Thr41/Tyr46) → β-catenin nuclear complex → fibrotic genes (Milara 2020 Thorax PMID:31801904)
- MUC1-KO mice PROTECTED from bleomycin fibrosis
- GO-201 peptide inhibitor effective in bleomycin model in vivo
- Pirfenidone partially works through MUC1-CT inhibition (Ballester 2020 PMID:32341751)
- NEW 2025: ADAM17/MUC1-CT/EZH2 axis — TGF-β induces ADAM17-dependent cleavage → MUC1-CT → EZH2 → H3K27me3 → repression of anti-fibrotic HO-1
- Dual role caveat: Muc1-KO WORSENS silicosis fibrosis (PMID:28916165) — extracellular domain is anti-inflammatory; must target activated CT specifically

**MUC1 vs MUC5B comparison:**
- Co-upregulated in honeycomb cysts (JCI Insight 2025 PMC12288964 spatial proteomics)
- Complementary not synergistic: MUC5B drives initiating epithelial injury; MUC1-CT amplifies fibroproliferative cascade
- Different modalities: MUC5B → ASO; MUC1 → CT peptide inhibitor or anti-MUC1-CT antibody

### 3. NTN4 (Netrin-4) Novel Biology Characterization

First comprehensive NTN4 analysis for IPF:

**Basic biology:**
- ~70 kDa secreted laminin-related protein; 50% identity to laminin β1 (not γ1 like other netrins)
- Highest tissue expression in lung (enrichment 26.6 — highest of any tissue)
- Highly expressed in **aberrant basaloid cells** — IPF-specific population absent from controls (IPFCellAtlas; Adams 2020 PMID:32832599)

**Receptor controversy:**
- Model 1: Binds neogenin, recruits UNC5B into signaling complex (Lejmi 2008 PNAS PMID:18719102)
- Model 2: Does NOT bind DCC/neogenin/UNC5 directly; binds laminin γ1 chain with high affinity (Reuten 2016 NatComm PMID:27901020, crystal structure 3.1Å)
- Disrupts pre-existing laminin networks non-enzymatically

**Anti-senescence/anti-inflammatory:**
- NTN4 knockdown → ↑CDKN1A (p21), ↑CDKN2A (p16), ↑SA-βgal in endothelial cells; rescued by exogenous NTN4 (PMID:33636396)
- NTN4 silencing → ↑VCAM-1, ↑ICAM-1, ↑monocyte adhesion, impaired barrier function

**Family context (NTN1):**
- NTN1 is definitively profibrotic from macrophages (Gao/Peng 2021 JCI PMID:33393489) — drives adrenergic nerve remodeling → noradrenaline → fibrosis
- NTN1 is PROTECTIVE from epithelium (ATS 2025 abstract) — cell-type paradox
- Alpha-1 blocker terazosin improved IPF patient survival (observational; PMID:36648147)
- NTN1 in liver fibrosis: autocrine in HSCs via UNC5B-Ca2+-SMAD2 (Zhao 2026 Adv Sci PMID:41514498)

**Druggability:**
- NP137 (anti-NTN1 mAb) in Phase I/II cancer (NETRIS Pharma) — validates netrin pathway druggability
- NTN4 is Pharos Tbio classification; no specific compounds
- Recombinant NTN4 protein available

**Critical gap:** No direct NTN4-fibrosis study exists in any organ. Highest novelty target in pipeline.

## Key Findings and Surprises

### 1. DPP9 Tissue Context Problem is Worse Than Expected
In kidney, DPP8/9 are pro-fibrotic (upregulated in CKD; inhibition is therapeutic). In lung IPF, DPP9 loss increases risk. In liver, hepatocyte-specific DPP9 KO is benign. This means DPP9 modulators CANNOT be used systemically — organ-specific delivery or downstream targeting (anti-IL-1β) is essential.

### 2. Pinocembrin is a DPP9 Activator — Proof-of-Concept for Correct Genetic Direction
This is the only published compound that increases DPP9 expression. It protects against lung injury via NLRP1 suppression. Needs medicinal chemistry optimization but validates the concept of DPP9 activation for IPF.

### 3. MUC1 Has Triple Evidence (Genetic + Functional + Pharmacological)
The convergence of GWAS signal → cell-type sc-eQTL in AT2 cells → MUC1-CT bioactivation → KO protection → GO-201 preclinical efficacy → pirfenidone mechanism makes MUC1 one of the most complete evidence packages in the pipeline. The dual role (extracellular anti-inflammatory vs CT pro-fibrotic) demands specificity in therapeutic targeting.

### 4. NTN4 is a Genuine Blank Canvas
No NTN4-fibrosis study exists in any organ. Its expression in aberrant basaloid cells (IPF-specific population), anti-senescence function in endothelium, and role in basement membrane disruption make it a compelling novel target. But directionality remains unclear.

### 5. Non-Telomere Pathways are Better Global Drug Targets
The All of Us analysis showing diminished telomere-PRS portability but preserved non-telomere-PRS portability suggests that mucin, inflammasome, TGF-β, and mTOR pathways may be more universally relevant for drug development across diverse populations.

## Limitations and Uncertainties

1. **DPP9 protein levels in IPF lung tissue are unmeasured** — scRNA shows DPP9 decreased in AT2/monocytes but INCREASED in myofibroblasts. Protein-level validation is critical.
2. **NTN4 directionality unknown** — GWAS says increased expression = risk, but NTN4 biology suggests it should be protective. May be context-dependent (basaloid cells vs endothelium).
3. **NTN4 receptor controversy unresolved** — competing structural and functional evidence for neogenin vs laminin binding
4. **Cross-ancestry data remains severely underpowered** — only 62 African-ancestry IPF cases (All of Us), 51 South Asian (GBMI). No dedicated non-European IPF GWAS exists.
5. **Pinocembrin is not a drug** — it's a natural flavonoid with unknown PK/PD. Proof-of-concept only.
6. **MUC1 rs4072037 confounds KL-6 levels** — genotype-dependent KL-6 baseline means clinical cutoffs need population calibration.
