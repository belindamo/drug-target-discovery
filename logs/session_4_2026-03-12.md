## Key Findings and Surprises

### 1. TERT Safety Concern Was Overstated

Real DepMap data shows TERT is NOT common essential — only 1.7% of cell lines are dependent. This is a significant correction that makes TERT activation strategies somewhat less risky than previously thought (though still challenging pharmacologically).

### 2. KIF15 Is the Real Safety Concern

100/1186 (8.4%) of cancer cell lines depend on KIF15 — the highest among all 21 targets. Since IPF requires KIF15 *activation* (decreased expression = risk), this creates a paradox: the cancer safety concern is about KIF15 *loss*, which aligns with the IPF direction. May actually indicate dual-indication potential rather than pure liability.

### 3. ACVRL1 Emerged as Most Tractable Target

With sotatercept FDA-approved for PAH (March 2024) and 10 OT tractability buckets, ACVRL1 has moved up in ranking. Its MR-confirmed causal link to IPF and the existence of clinical-grade compounds make it highly actionable, though the HHT phenotype (telangiectasia/epistaxis) from ACVRL1 loss-of-function is a safety concern.

### 4. DPP9 Inhibitor Compound Numbering Corrected

The key DPP9-selective inhibitors are compounds **42 and 47** (not compound 6e as previously noted). Compound 42 has 175x DPP9/DPP8 selectivity with nanomolar affinity. F253 identified as the key selectivity residue (ChemMedChem 2025).

### 5. MUC1 Is a New Genetically Validated Target

Chin et al. 2025 identified MUC1 (rs9426886, 1q22) as a novel IPF GWAS locus with eQTL colocalization (PP=0.616). This is distinct from MUC5B and implicates transmembrane mucin biology. KL-6 (a MUC1 epitope) is an established IPF progression biomarker — the genetic finding provides causal anchor for this biomarker-to-biology link.

### 6. Bexotegrast Failure Supports Genetic Target Selection (Partially)

The bexotegrast discontinuation (integrins, no genetic support) adds to the evidence that targets without GWAS backing may face higher attrition. However, nerandomilast (PDE4B, also no genetic support) succeeded, and the 2026 MR preprint shows MR alone doesn't predict Phase 2 success. The relationship between genetic support and clinical success is more nuanced than "genetic support = success."

## Limitations and Uncertainties

1. **DepMap scores reflect cancer cell line biology** — may not predict normal lung tissue toxicity. A gene essential in 8% of cancers (KIF15) might be perfectly safe to modulate in fibrotic fibroblasts.
2. **Open Targets tractability is based on the druggable genome framework** — new modalities (ASO, CRISPR therapeutics, molecular glues) may make "untractable" targets accessible.
3. **Chin 2025 is a medRxiv preprint** — the 3 new loci await peer review. The recessive model GWAS (2026) is also a preprint.
4. **MUC1 eQTL colocalization is modest** (PP=0.616) — below our standard threshold of 0.8. Included because KL-6 biomarker data provides independent biological support.
5. **DPP9 therapeutic direction remains complex** — DPP9 inhibitors activate NLRP1 inflammasome (pro-inflammatory), but IPF risk allele reduces DPP9 expression. Partial inhibition may be therapeutic; complete inhibition would be neonatally lethal.
