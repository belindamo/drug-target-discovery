### 1. DPP9 Therapeutic Strategy — Hepatocyte-KO Evidence and 4-Strategy Framework

Created `/workspace/drug-target-discovery/DPP9_therapeutic_strategy.md` integrating new evidence not in Sessions 5-9:

**Key new evidence:**
- Hepatocyte-specific DPP9 KO is tolerated (ScienceDirect 2025; ASCO 2025): viable mice, no pyroptosis, autophagy dominant (4/4 markers↑), NLRP1 was "minor mechanism" — demonstrates tissue-specific DPP9 loss is survivable
- DPP9 S759A catalytic-dead mutant only partially rescues NLRP1 (PMID:31525884) — proves graded inflammasome response, not binary
- FAP-DPP9 non-enzymatic interaction (PMID:32273729): FAP physically interacts with DPP9; FAP↑→DPP9↓→EMT; DPP9 overexpression reverses FAP-induced proliferation/migration
- Global KO rescue: specifically IL-1R (not IL-18) haploinsufficiency rescues — confirms IL-1β is the critical effector

**Formal 4-strategy framework:**
1. Anti-IL-1β (PRIMARY): anakinra/canakinumab; genetically aligned; approved; never tested in IPF
2. DPP9 gene therapy: restores expression; requires development
3. FAP-DPP9 axis: monitors cardiac fibrosis data
4. Partial inhibition: REJECTED (wrong direction)

### 2. MUC1 Deep Functional Profile

Created MUC1 section in `/workspace/drug-target-discovery/novel_target_characterizations.md` with specific new data points:

- MUC1-CT phosphorylation at **T1224 and Y1229** — specific residues increased in IPF (Milara 2020, PMID:31801904)
- **Nuclear complex**: pSmad2/3 + β-catenin + MUC1-CT translocates to nucleus → fibrotic gene activation
- **MUC1 siRNA** blocks TGFβ1-induced EMT — proving MUC1 is required (not bystander)
- **Galectin-3** bridges MUC1-C to EGFR/FGFR3/PDGFRα/TGFβR — connects to nintedanib targets
- **GO-203 nanoparticle formulation** developed for improved PK (peptide limitation addressed)
- **Pirfenidone MUC1-CT mechanism**: anti-fibrotic effect partially via MUC1-CT phosphorylation inhibition (PMID:32341751)
- **Caveat**: MUC1-KO in silicosis model paradoxically worsened fibrosis (PMID:29017036) — context-dependent

### 3. NTN4 and SLC6A6 — First Comprehensive Profiles

Created NTN4 and SLC6A6 sections in novel_target_characterizations.md:

**NTN4 — Key distinctions from NTN1:**
| Feature | NTN1 | NTN4 |
|---|---|---|
| Homology | Laminin γ1 chain | Laminin β1 chain (50%) |
| Receptors | DCC, UNC5, neogenin | None (binds laminin γ1 directly) |
| IPF role | Pro-fibrotic (macrophage-derived) | Unknown (likely protective in EC) |
| GWAS | No | Yes (rs7957346, Chin 2025) |

**SLC6A6 — Mitochondrial biology:**
- NatMetab 2026: mitochondrial localization + taurine import for tRNA modifications
- Exogenous taurine CANNOT rescue SLC6A6 loss (transporter essential, not substrate)
- Multi-organ fibrosis: cardiac + kidney in KO models
- Strongest Chin 2025 GWAS signal (p=2.23e-11) but no IPF functional data

## Key Findings

1. **Hepatocyte-specific DPP9 KO tolerance** proves tissue-specific DPP9 loss is survivable with non-inflammasome mechanisms dominant — reshapes the safety narrative for DPP9-related interventions
2. **MUC1 functional evidence exceeds most higher-ranked targets** — KO protection, in vivo pharmacology, existing drug mechanism all validated; only limited by subthreshold genetic coloc (PP=0.616)
3. **NTN4 is biologically distinct from NTN1** — cannot extrapolate pro-fibrotic NTN1 findings; NTN4 operates through laminin network disruption and endothelial homeostasis
4. **SLC6A6 mitochondrial import** eliminates taurine supplementation as therapeutic strategy
5. **DPP9 is the network hub** connecting 3 of 6 pathway clusters

## Limitations

1. Hepatocyte ≠ fibroblast DPP9 biology
2. MUC1 coloc PP=0.616 subthreshold
3. Network analysis manually curated
4. NTN4 direction paradox unresolved
5. All work from literature extraction
