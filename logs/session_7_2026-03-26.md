Removing a single copy of IL-1R rescues mice to normal adulthood. This is the single most important study supporting anti-IL-1β therapy in IPF: haploinsufficiency of any downstream inflammasome component (NLRP1, ASC, GSDMD, IL-1R — but NOT IL-18) rescues the phenotype. This proves the therapeutic window is wide.

- **NLRP1 vs. CARD8 mechanistic distinction** (Gai et al., ACS Chem Biol 2019, PMC: 6862324): DPP9 restrains NLRP1 via both binding AND enzyme activity, but CARD8 via enzyme activity only. DPP8/9 inhibitors disrupt the NLRP1-DPP9 interaction directly but do NOT disrupt the CARD8-DPP9 interaction. This has implications for selective activation of different inflammasome subtypes.

- **DPP9-KEAP1 redox axis** (Mena et al., JBC 2024, PMID: 39615677): DPP9 and KEAP1 form a mutually inhibitory complex. DPP9 loss → impaired KEAP1 sequestration → NRF2 degradation → reduced antioxidant defenses. Adds a third mechanistic axis to the DPP9 model (beyond inflammasome + EMT).

- **Pinocembrin as DPP9 activator** (Immunol Res 2024): First reported compound that activates (rather than inhibits) DPP9 expression, suppressing NLRP1 inflammasome. Natural product with polypharmacology, but proof-of-concept that DPP9 activation is achievable.

- **Therapeutic strategy matrix**: Formal ranking of 5 approaches — anti-IL-1β is #1 (perfect genetic alignment, approved drugs, never tested in IPF). DPP9 activation is conceptually ideal but pharmacologically premature.

- **Proposed clinical trial design**: Canakinumab (anti-IL-1β mAb, q8w SC) in DPP9 risk genotype-enriched early IPF; FVC primary endpoint at 52 weeks; BAL IL-1β as biomarker. Referenced CANTOS trial (PMID: 28845751) as precedent for anti-IL-1β benefit in lung tissue.

## Key Findings

### 1. The IL-1R Haploinsufficiency Rescue Is the Strongest Genetic Argument for Anti-IL-1 Therapy in IPF

The Harapas 2022 experiment (removing 1 copy of Il-1r rescues DPP9-null mice) provides the most direct evidence possible: partial IL-1 blockade is sufficient to compensate for DPP9 loss. For IPF patients carrying the DPP9 risk allele (rs12610495-G, ~25% EUR), anti-IL-1β therapy (anakinra/canakinumab) would directly address the genetic mechanism. No other IPF drug target has this level of genetic-to-clinical alignment.

### 2. DPP9 Has Three Mechanistic Axes, Not One

- **Axis 1**: NLRP1/CARD8 inflammasome suppression (immune cells) — most studied
- **Axis 2**: EMT/fibroblast regulation via FAP and ZEB1 (fibroblasts) — discovered 2017-2024
- **Axis 3**: KEAP1-NRF2 redox sensing (ubiquitous) — discovered 2024

The triple-mechanism model explains why DPP9's colocalization is fibroblast-specific (PP.H4=0.989): the fibroblast-autonomous EMT function (Axis 2) may be the primary IPF-relevant mechanism, with inflammasome activation (Axis 1) as a secondary amplifier.

### 3. AKAP13→ROCK Pathway Now Has Active IPF Clinical Data

Zelasudil (RXC007) is the first ROCK inhibitor entering an IPF trial. The AKAP13 GWAS locus (rs62025270, OR=1.27) provides the genetic rationale for this mechanism. If zelasudil shows efficacy, AKAP13 will become the **first genetically validated IPF target with a corresponding clinical trial**.

---

## Limitations

1. **Harapas rescue is in mice, not humans**: The IL-1R haploinsufficiency rescue was demonstrated in Dpp9 S759A/S759A mice. Human DPP9 variant carriers may have different responses due to species-specific NLRP1 biology (human has 1 NLRP1 gene; mice have 3 paralogs).
2. **Pinocembrin polypharmacology**: The reported DPP9 activator (pinocembrin) is a natural product flavonoid with numerous targets. DPP9 activation may be indirect. No selective DPP9 activator exists.
3. **Zelasudil clinical hold history**: The prior partial clinical hold suggests safety concerns that may limit zelasudil's IPF development even if the hold is lifted.
4. **DPP9-KEAP1 axis**: The mutual inhibition between DPP9 and KEAP1 is from a single 2024 publication. Replication needed.
