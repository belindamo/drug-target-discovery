# Clinical Gap Analysis: IPF Genetic Evidence vs. Current Drug Pipeline

*Updated Session 5 (2026-03-26) — DPP9 therapeutic deep-dive, MUC1 functional validation, NTN4 biology, pathway network analysis, pipeline updates*

## Overview

This analysis compares drug targets supported by human genetic evidence (GWAS, TWAS, colocalization, MR) against targets currently being pursued in IPF clinical trials. The goal is to identify:

1. **Genetically validated targets already in trials** (concordance)
2. **Genetically validated targets NOT in trials** (novel opportunities — the "gap")
3. **Targets in trials WITHOUT genetic support** (potential risk)

### Major Pipeline Failures (2024-2025) — None Had Genetic Support

**Bexotegrast (PLN-74809)** — dual αvβ6/αvβ1 integrin inhibitor **DISCONTINUED** June 2025. BEACON-IPF stopped by DSMB: increased IPF-related AEs (worsening, hospitalization, death). Week 12 efficacy (72 mL) vanished by week 24. Pliant: 45% workforce reduction. **No genetic support.**

**GB0139 / Olitigaltin** — Galectin-3 inhaled inhibitor. Phase 2b GALACTIC-1 **FAILED**: no target engagement (galectin-3 levels increased in both arms). GB0139 FVC -316.6 mL vs placebo -127.4 mL (WORSE). Galecto dropped IPF. **No genetic support.**

**Zinpentraxin alfa / PRM-151** — Pentraxin-2 recombinant. Phase 3 STARSCAPE terminated for **futility**: FVC placebo -214.89 vs drug -235.72 mL (P=0.54). Phase 2 effect driven by 2 placebo outliers. Roche $400M write-off. **No genetic support.**

**Pattern**: 3/5 high-profile IPF failures in 2024-2025 lacked genetic support (bexotegrast, GB0139, zinpentraxin). 2/5 genetically unsupported drugs succeeded (nerandomilast, treprostinil). Overall failure rate without genetics: ~60%.

### Session 6 Pipeline Updates (March 2026)

**TETON-1 (Inhaled Treprostinil):** 598 patients enrolled (Jan 2025 completion). All patients must complete 52-week follow-up; initial results expected H1 2026. If positive, sNDA planned combining TETON-1 + TETON-2 data for IPF indication.

**ALOFT-IPF Phase 3 (Admilparant):** Now enrolling across >30 countries, >400 sites. Novel 2-cohort design: Cohort 1 (~60 pts, single-blind) evaluates 120 mg safety (syncopal episodes at 4 wks); Cohort 2 (~1,125 pts, double-blind) is registrational (60 mg or 120 mg vs placebo, FVC at 52 wks). Completion expected October 2026.

**Vixarelimab (MOONSCAPE Phase 2):** First OSMRβ-targeting therapy in IPF. OSMRβ mediates both IL-31 and oncostatin M (OSM) signaling, both implicated in fibrosis/inflammation. As of Sept 2024: 149 IPF patients randomized in Cohort 1 (70.5% male, median age 73.0, median FVC% 76.4%). 44.3% on background antifibrotics. No genetic support — OSMRβ is not a GWAS locus.

**Zelasudil (RXC007) Phase 2a:** Redx Pharma's ROCK2-selective inhibitor had its partial clinical hold LIFTED in March 2026. This is the **first ROCK inhibitor in an active IPF trial**. Notably, AKAP13 (GWAS OR=1.27) is a RhoA-GEF that feeds directly into the RhoA→ROCK pathway. Zelasudil is thus the **first IPF trial drug with indirect genetic support from our pipeline** (AKAP13 GWAS → RhoA → ROCK2). This reduces the "gap" — one genetically-supported pathway is now entering clinical testing.

**Compound 6e (DPP9 selective inhibitor):** Full publication now available — J Med Chem 2025, 68(21):23163-23184 (Filippi et al., Antwerp). Key PK: high oral bioavailability, long microsomal and in vivo half-life in rats. Unprecedented pyroptosis-inducing potency. DPP9-to-DPP8 selectivity maintained. However, per Session 5 paradox analysis, this compound is WRONG direction for IPF (would worsen inflammasome activation). Primarily relevant for AML immunotherapy.

**ADS032 (dual NLRP1/NLRP3 inhibitor):** Adiso Therapeutics clinical-stage compound. First-in-class dual inflammasome inhibitor that directly binds NACHT domains of both NLRP1 and NLRP3 (CTI 2023, PMID: 37360982). Reduces IL-1β in human macrophages and bronchial epithelial cells. In vivo: reduced pulmonary inflammation in silicosis model; protected against lethal influenza A. Being developed for inflammatory lung diseases. **Genetically rational for IPF** via DPP9→NLRP1 pathway. Represents a next-generation approach beyond IL-1β blockade — addressing the inflammasome directly.

### Major Pipeline Success: Inhaled Treprostinil

**Inhaled treprostinil (Tyvaso)** showed a striking 95.6 mL FVC benefit in TETON-2 (NEJM March 2026) — the largest treatment effect in any IPF trial. However, this target also **lacks genetic support**. If validated in TETON-1, this would be the second major IPF approval without genetic backing, complicating the narrative that genetic support is necessary for success.

## Key Insights (Updated)

### 1. DPP9 Has Become the #1 Actionable Novel Target

Since Session 3, the druggability landscape for DPP9 has transformed:

- **Selective nanomolar inhibitors** are now published (J Med Chem 2023, 2025)
- **Oral bioavailability** demonstrated in rats (compound 6e, 2025)
- **Fibroblast-specific colocalization** (PP.H4=0.989) provides cell-type rationale
- **F253** identified as key selectivity residue (ChemMedChem 2025)
- Previous concern about DPP4 off-target effects is resolved by 175x selectivity

**Recommendation**: DPP9-selective inhibitor → IPF proof-of-concept trial. This target has the strongest combined genetic evidence + druggability of any genetically-supported IPF target not currently in clinical development.

### 1b. DPP9 Therapeutic Paradox → IL-1β as Genetically-Aligned Downstream Target

However, a critical mechanistic puzzle has emerged with DPP9 (detailed in Session 5 deep-dive):

**The Paradox:**
- GWAS genetics: ↓DPP9 expression = ↑IPF risk (loss-of-function harmful)
- Pharmacology: Only **inhibitors** exist; no activators available
- Mechanism: DPP9 normally **represses NLRP1/CARD8 inflammasome** (Nature 2021 PMID:33731929)
- Consequence: DPP9 **inhibitors would activate** inflammasome → IL-1β release → **worsen fibrosis**

**Solution**: The causal chain is **DPP9 loss → NLRP1 activation → IL-1β release → myofibroblast differentiation → fibrosis**. Rather than impossible DPP9 activation, target the **final common path: IL-1β inhibition**.

**IL-1β Inhibitors as Genetically-Aligned Alternative:**

Three clinical-grade IL-1 pathway inhibitors are approved and well-tolerated:
- **Anakinra** (Kineret): IL-1 receptor antagonist, daily SC injection, approved RA/SJIA/autoinflammatory syndromes
- **Canakinumab** (Ilaris): Anti-IL-1β monoclonal antibody, q8w SC, approved periodic fever syndromes
- **Rilonacept** (Arcalyst): Soluble IL-1 receptor trap, approved periodic fever syndromes

**Safety profile**: >10,000 patients treated globally; well-tolerated, immunosuppression risk lower than TNF inhibitors.

**The Opportunity**: **No IL-1β inhibitor has ever been tested in IPF**, despite:
1. NLRP3/AIM2 inflammasome activation being a consensus mechanism in IPF pathogenesis (reviews 2024-2025)
2. IL-1β being a master pro-fibrotic cytokine (drives TGF-β activation, myofibroblast differentiation, neutrophil recruitment)
3. These drugs being approved, safe, and shelf-ready
4. Strong genetic rationale (DPP9 GWAS → NLRP1 pathway → IL-1β)

**Recommended trial design:**
- **Patient enrichment**: High baseline IL-1β (BAL fluid or plasma) to select IL-1β-driven IPF phenotype (may represent ~30-40% of IPF)
- **Intervention**: Anakinra (simplest, daily SC) or canakinumab (less frequent dosing) + standard care (pirfenidone ± nintedanib)
- **Comparator**: Standard care alone
- **Biomarker endpoints**: IL-1β, IL-18, pro-IL-1β, inflammasome markers (ASC, cleaved caspase-1) in sputum/BAL
- **Functional endpoints**: FVC change, symptom score, exercise tolerance

**Why overlooked?** IPF drug development has historically focused on TGF-β pathway (pirfenidone) or angiogenesis (nintedanib, treprostinil). Anti-inflammatory therapy was viewed skeptically (concern that IL-1 is needed for acute repair). But chronic IPF is a disease of aberrant inflammation + fibrosis, and IL-1 may be more pro-fibrotic than pro-reparative in this context.

**Rank in opportunity list**: **#2 actionable target** (after DPP9 selective inhibitor, alongside IL-7). Requires no drug development; requires only clinical trial funding.

### 2. IL-7 Now Has Preclinical AND Clinical Support

IL-7's antifibrotic mechanism is now well-characterized:

- Inhibits TGF-β production AND signaling in fibroblasts
- Requires JAK1/STAT1 pathway → increases Smad7 (TGF-β pathway inhibitor)
- Reduces bleomycin-induced pulmonary fibrosis in vivo
- CYT107 is safe in pulmonary patients (COVID trial, JCI Insight 2025)
- Now tested in lung disease (IMPULSE-7 for MAC lung disease)

**This is the most "ready" repurposing opportunity**: existing clinical-grade drug + genetic support + preclinical mechanism.

### 3. The mTOR Pathway Deserves Another Chance

The everolimus failure in IPF was likely due to **mTORC2 inhibition**, not mTOR pathway irrelevance:

- Sirolimus (which inhibits mTORC1 > mTORC2) was better tolerated in a pilot trial
- Two independent GWAS loci (DEPTOR, NPRL3) converge on mTORC1 negative regulation
- TGF-β suppresses DEPTOR → mTOR hyperactivation → collagen synthesis

**Sirolimus, not everolimus**, should be tested in an adequately powered IPF trial.

### 4. Bexotegrast Failure Supports Genetic Target Selection

Bexotegrast (integrins, no genetic support) was discontinued for safety. Meanwhile, nerandomilast (PDE4B, no genetic support) succeeded. This shows genetic support is not the only path to efficacy, but the integrin failure — in a target class with no GWAS backing — is cautionary.

### 5. ATP11A Emerges as a Stronger Target

New single-cell data shows ATP11A is upregulated specifically in macrophages and myofibroblasts during IPF, linking to TGF-β/EMT. Its PP.H4=0.996 colocalization in smoker lung eQTL is among the strongest in our pipeline. As a P4-ATPase, it belongs to a druggable enzyme family.

This suggests **non-telomere IPF pathways (mucin, inflammasome, TGF-β, mTOR) may be better global drug targets than telomere biology**.

## Session 5 Update (2026-03-26): Major New Findings

### 1. IL-1β Blockade — The Biggest Translational Gap in IPF

**No clinical trial of anakinra or canakinumab in IPF exists as of March 2026**, despite:
- DPP9 GWAS signal (p=1.7e-12) with fibroblast-specific colocalization (PP.H4=0.989)
- DPP9 KO neonatal lethality rescued by single Il-1r allele deletion (PMID: 36112693)
- IL-1β is a direct pro-fibrotic cytokine driving TGF-β expression and myofibroblast differentiation
- Anakinra has been successfully inhaled in cystic fibrosis models (improved efficacy over systemic delivery)
- NLRP3 inflammasome activation confirmed in IPF macrophages (enhanced mtROS-dependent IL-1β release)
- ADS032, first dual NLRP1/NLRP3 inhibitor, reduces pulmonary inflammation in lung models (CTI 2023, PMID: 37360982)

**Proposed trial:** Anakinra SC daily + SOC vs. SOC in early/mild IPF, stratified by DPP9 rs12610495 genotype and baseline BAL IL-1β.

### 2. MUC1 Upgraded to Tier 1 — Functional Fibrotic Driver

MUC1-CT is a validated functional mediator of IPF pathogenesis (not merely a biomarker):
- TGFβ1 phosphorylates MUC1-CT (T1224) → forms nuclear complex with β-catenin/pSmad2/3 → activates fibrotic genes
- MUC1 KO mice protected from bleomycin-induced fibrosis
- GO-201/GO-203 peptide inhibitors reduce fibrosis in vivo
- KL-6 (MUC1 glycoprotein) promotes fibroblast migration, proliferation, survival (more potent than PDGF)
- Pirfenidone partially works via MUC1-CT inhibition (PMID: 29456657)

### 3. NTN4 — Novel Vascular/Endothelial Pathway in IPF

Netrin-4 (12q23.1, Chin 2025) connects IPF to basement membrane biology and endothelial senescence:
- Disrupts laminin networks non-enzymatically (Nature Comms 2016)
- Anti-senescence and anti-inflammatory in endothelial cells
- 26.6-fold enriched in lung tissue
- IPF risk allele also associated with decreased lung function (FEV1/FVC GWAS)

### 4. DPP9-KEAP1-NRF2 Axis — New Redox Dimension

DPP9 forms a mutually inhibitory complex with KEAP1 (JBC 2024, PMID: 39615677):
- DPP9 loss → reduced KEAP1 sequestration → NRF2 degradation → oxidative stress
- Connects inflammasome pathway to redox imbalance in IPF
- NRF2 activators (dimethyl fumarate, sulforaphane) may partially compensate

### TOP 5 Actionable Opportunities — Final Revised Ranking

1. **MUC1-CT inhibition**: GWAS + KO protection + in vivo pharmacology + existing drug mechanism (pirfenidone). Next-gen MUC1-CT inhibitors (GO-203 nanoparticles or small molecules) could surpass pirfenidone.

2. **IL-7 (CYT107) for IPF**: MR-supported, clinical-grade recombinant available, preclinical anti-fibrotic evidence, 0% DepMap liability. Ready for proof-of-concept trial.

3. **Anti-IL-1β (anakinra/canakinumab) for IPF**: Genetically aligned to DPP9 locus via NLRP1 inflammasome. FDA-approved drugs; never tested in IPF — the single largest translational gap.

4. **Sirolimus for IPF**: Two independent GWAS loci (DEPTOR + NPRL3) converge on mTORC1. Pilot trial showed fibrocyte suppression. Adequately powered RCT justified.

5. **ACVRL1 pathway (sotatercept) for PH-IPF**: 10 OT tractability buckets, FDA-approved for PAH, MR-confirmed. Pulmonary hypertension in IPF is a major driver of mortality.
