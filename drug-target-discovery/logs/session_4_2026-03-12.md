### 4. Cross-Validated Top Targets Against 2025-2026 Literature

**Major new GWAS findings:**

- Chin et al. 2025: 3 novel loci (MUC1, NTN4, SLC6A6) from largest clinically-curated IPF GWAS (5,159 cases)
- Kousathanas et al. 2025 (eBioMedicine/Lancet): Novel MCL1 locus (protective); ANGPTL7 rare variant (OR=28.8); 6 shared IPF-COVID loci confirmed
- Recessive model GWAS 2026: 5 novel signals including PMF1, EPN3

**Major clinical trial updates:**

- **Nerandomilast**: FDA approved Oct 2025 (IPF) + Dec 2025 (PPF). FIBRONEER-IPF: 68.8 mL FVC benefit (NEJM, PMID: 40387033)
- **Inhaled treprostinil**: TETON-2 positive — 95.6 mL FVC benefit (NEJM March 2026). Largest IPF trial effect ever. sNDA planned H2 2026.
- **Bexotegrast**: DISCONTINUED June 2025 (safety signal: worsening IPF events). No genetic support.
- **Admilparant**: Phase 2 positive (60 mg reduced FVC decline); Phase 3 ALOFT-IPF ongoing (completion Oct 2026)

**DPP9 druggability breakthrough:**

- Compound 42: Ki ~nM, 175-fold selectivity over DPP8 (J Med Chem 2023)
- Compound 6e: Orally bioavailable, long half-life, low-nanomolar DPP9 inhibitor (J Med Chem 2025)
- F253 identified as key DPP9 selectivity residue (ChemMedChem 2025)
- This transforms DPP9 from "concept" to "actionable" target

**IL-7 antifibrotic evidence discovered:**

- Published preclinical study: IL-7 inhibits TGF-β production AND signaling in fibroblasts via JAK1/STAT1/Smad7
- Reduces bleomycin-induced pulmonary fibrosis in vivo (independent of IFN-γ)
- CYT107 safe in 109 critically ill COVID patients (JCI Insight 2025; no cytokine storm, no pulmonary toxicity)
- IMPULSE-7 Phase 2 for MAC lung disease (2025)

**mTOR pathway update:**

- Sirolimus crossover pilot in IPF (JCI Insight 2023): tolerated, suppressed circulating fibrocytes
- Sirolimus > everolimus for IPF (differential mTORC2 inhibition explains everolimus failure)
- Genetic rationale strengthened: both DEPTOR and NPRL3 converge on mTORC1 negative regulation

**ATP11A emerging evidence:**

- 2025 HGG Advances study: ATP11A upregulated in macrophages and myofibroblasts during IPF
- Opposite direction for IPF vs COVID-19 (consistent with shared genetic locus)
- PP.H4=0.996 in smoker lung eQTL (strongest colocalization of any non-MUC5B/DSP gene)

**GLIS3 — new functional evidence (not GWAS):**

- Nature 2026: Bidirectional CRISPR screens identified GLIS3 as master fibrotic transcription factor
- Controls IL11, LIF, FAP, MMP2 — key fibrotic effectors
- Not a GWAS locus but important functional context

## Key Findings This Session

1. **DPP9 is now the #1 actionable novel target for IPF** — orally bioavailable, DPP9-selective, nanomolar inhibitors exist; fibroblast-specific genetic signal; no IPF trial despite strong genetics. This is the single most important finding of the pipeline.

1. **IL-7 (CYT107) is the most "ready" repurposing opportunity** — genetic support (MR OR=0.67), preclinical antifibrotic mechanism (TGF-β inhibition via Smad7), and clinical safety data (>650 patients). No new drug development needed.

1. **Bexotegrast failure validates genetic target selection hypothesis** — the integrin inhibitor (no GWAS support) was discontinued for safety while showing initial efficacy that waned. Genetic targets may offer more durable disease modification.

1. **However, inhaled treprostinil's success (also no genetic support) is a counterpoint** — 95.6 mL FVC benefit in TETON-2 would be the largest IPF treatment effect if replicated. Genetic support increases probability of success but is not the only path.

1. **Cross-ancestry data reveals important stratification considerations** — TERT is the most globally generalizable target; MUC5B-based approaches would under-represent non-European patients; EAS-specific signals (PSKH1, GPR157) require targeted development.

1. **ATP11A has quietly become one of the strongest coloc signals** — PP.H4=0.996 in smoker lung eQTL, plus cell-type-specific upregulation in macrophages and myofibroblasts. P4-ATPase family is druggable. Worth tool compound investment.

## Limitations and Uncertainties

1. **DepMap Chronos scores obtained from 24Q4 bulk data** — Real scores now integrated for all 21 targets. RTEL1 identified as common essential (Chronos=-0.571); TERT reclassified as strongly selective (only 1.7% dependent lines). NPRL3 has limited DepMap coverage (0/45 lines in subset tested).

1. **TWAS z-scores from Gao 2020 still incomplete** — supplementary tables not fully extractable via web search. Many entries still show "NA" for z-score and R². Would need direct download of supplementary Excel files.

1. **New GWAS loci (Chin 2025, recessive 2026) are from preprints** — MUC1, NTN4, SLC6A6, PMF1, EPN3 need peer review confirmation. Not added to gwas_loci.csv until published.

1. **MR effect sizes for some genes are imprecise** — multiple studies report FDR-adjusted significance but not exact betas/ORs for all genes. MR results for GREM1, UGT1A6, CDH2, TDO2 lack detailed effect sizes.

1. **DPP9 selective inhibitors tested in AML/HIV context, not fibrosis** — pyroptosis induction is the studied mechanism. IPF-specific DPP9 pharmacology (NLRP1 inflammasome regulation in fibroblasts) needs validation.

1. **IL-7 preclinical fibrosis evidence is old** — key study uses bleomycin model (standard but imperfect IPF model). Modern IPF mouse models may give different results.
