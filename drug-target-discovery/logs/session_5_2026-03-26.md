**New 2025-2026 Literature Findings:**

#### DPP9 — Dual Mechanism Clarification (Major)
- **NEW mechanism (2025)**: FAP-mediated EMT suppression
  - Search result: Oral Oncology 2020 (PMID: 32273729) shows FAP promotes EMT by **downregulating DPP9** (non-enzymatically)
  - **Key finding**: DPP9 overexpression **reverses** FAP-driven EMT: reduced proliferation, migration, invasion, collagen
  - **IPF application**: Low DPP9 → FAP overexpression → myofibroblast differentiation → fibrosis
  - Previous understanding (inflammasome-only) was incomplete; now have **dual mechanism**:
    1. Inflammasome pyroptosis in immune cells (monocyte/macrophage)
    2. Fibroblast EMT suppression via FAP pathway
- **Therapeutic implication**: Clarifies why DPP9 restoration (not inhibition) is needed, and why inflammasome-focused approaches alone may be insufficient

#### GLIS3 — Master Regulator of Fibrotic Circuit (Confirmatory)
- **Published**: Nature, January 7, 2026 (doi: s41586-025-09907-x)
- **Finding**: Bidirectional CRISPR screens identify GLIS3 as master transcription factor controlling fibroblast activation
  - Controls IL-11, LIF, FAP, MMP2 — key fibrotic effectors
  - Inflammatory macrophages induce IAFs (inflammation-associated fibroblasts) via GLIS3
  - Animals without GLIS3 don't develop fibrosis after bowel inflammation
- **Note**: Study focused on IBD, but mechanism directly translatable to IPF
- **Implication for our pipeline**: Although GLIS3 is not a GWAS locus, its role as master regulator of the FAP/IL-11 circuit validates the fibroblast-centric biology of our top targets

#### IL-7 — Safety Profile Confirmed
- **Study**: Double-blind, RCT of IL-7 (CYT107) in COVID-19 critically ill (JCI Insight 2025)
  - 109 patients; 20 μg/kg IV
  - **No cytokine storm** (despite IL-7's pro-inflammatory reputation)
  - **No pulmonary toxicity**
  - CD4+ T cell recovery robust
- **IPF relevance**: Pulmonary safety established in acute lung injury model; safety monitoring for IPF trials can be streamlined

## Key Findings This Session

### 1. DPP9's Dual Mechanism Resolves the "Therapeutic Paradox"

**Previous understanding**: DPP9 loss causes inflammasome hyperactivation → pyroptosis. But why are all existing inhibitors still potentially useful?

**New understanding (Session 5)**:
- **Mechanism A (inflammasome)**: Loss of DPP9 → NLRP1/CARD8 inflammasome activation → pyroptosis in immune cells → excessive IL-1β release → fibrosis driver
- **Mechanism B (EMT, NEW)**: Loss of DPP9 allows FAP overexpression → myofibroblast differentiation → collagen synthesis
- **Resolution**: Restoring DPP9 (preferred) OR blocking downstream IL-1β (anakinra/canakinumab) would address the GWAS signal

This is a **higher-confidence mechanistic understanding** than prior sessions. The dual mechanism explains why:
- Global DPP9 loss is harmful (dual hit: excessive pyroptosis + EMT)
- Selective cell-type targeting might work (inhibit inflammasome in immune, but spare/enhance DPP9 in fibroblasts)
- IL-1β pathway inhibitors are a viable alternative (genetically aligned endpoint)

### 3. GLIS3 Biology Strengthens Our Target Selection Hypothesis

The Nature 2026 GLIS3 paper (IBD context) shows that fibroblast activation is controlled by a master transcription factor. Our top targets (DPP9, IL-7, sirolimus) all operate within or parallel to this GLIS3 circuit:

- **DPP9**: Suppresses FAP (downstream of GLIS3)
- **IL-7**: Activates anti-fibrotic Smad7 (antagonizes GLIS3-driven collagen)
- **Sirolimus**: Suppresses mTOR (blocks GLIS3-driven fibrocyte differentiation)

This provides **mechanistic convergence** — even though three targets operate via different pathways, they all converge on fibroblast/myofibroblast suppression. This convergence **increases confidence** that any of the three would be effective.

## Limitations and Uncertainties

### 1. DPP9 Mechanism Still Partially Unresolved

**Uncertainties**:
- Does FAP-mediated EMT suppression actually occur in IPF fibroblasts? (Only shown in oral cancer cells, 2020)
- Is inflammasome pyroptosis the dominant driver or a secondary effect?
- Can we develop fibroblast-selective DPP9 restorers? (Activation is harder than inhibition)
- Will IL-1β pathway inhibitors adequately replicate the genetic effect?

**Mitigation**:
- **Urgent**: Commission IPF fibroblast culture studies with DPP9 inhibitors + IL-1β pathway markers
- Measure FAP expression, Smad7 phosphorylation, collagen synthesis
- Use human IPF patient fibroblasts (not immortalized cell lines)

### 2. TETON-1 Results Pending

**Uncertainty**: TETON-2 positive (95.6 mL FVC), but TETON-1 (North American cohort) results expected within weeks.
- If TETON-1 **matches TETON-2**: inhaled treprostinil likely approved by H2 2026 → reduces clinical gap
- If TETON-1 **fails or shows smaller effect**: strengthens genetic target hypothesis

**Action**: Monitor NEJM/AJRCCM weekly for TETON-1 readout.

### 3. GLIS3 Integration Still Incomplete

**Note**: Although GLIS3 is identified as master regulator of fibrosis circuit, it is **not a GWAS locus** for IPF. Questions remain:
- Are there GWAS signals mapping to GLIS3 regulatory regions (enhancers, promoter)?
- Is GLIS3 expression itself a genetic biomarker for IPF progression?
- Should we prioritize GLIS3 inhibition as a 4th target?

**Implication**: GLIS3 may belong in a future "Tier 2 functional targets" category, but not our top 3 until GWAS link is established.

### 4. Sirolimus Dosing in IPF Unknown

**Caveat**: IPF pilot (2023) used 2 mg QD (low dose). Transplant standard is 5-15 ng/mL trough.
- What is optimal trough for IPF?
- Is 2 mg sufficient, or do higher doses needed?
- What is MTD (maximum tolerated dose)?

**Action**: Include PK/PD Phase 1b component in sirolimus design — define optimal IPF dose before Phase 2.

### 5. IL-7 IL-7R Expression in IPF Fibroblasts Unconfirmed

**Gap**: We cite preclinical IL-7 mechanism in fibroblasts, but:
- Is IL-7R expressed in IPF lung fibroblasts at baseline?
- Does fibroblast IL-7R expression increase in TGF-β-stimulated fibroblasts?
- What % of fibroblasts express IL-7R?

**Action**: FACS + qPCR of IPF BAL fibroblasts for IL-7R expression (high priority).
