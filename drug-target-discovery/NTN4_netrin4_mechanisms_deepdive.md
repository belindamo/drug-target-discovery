# NTN4 (Netrin-4) Biology in IPF — Mechanisms and Therapeutic Implications (Session 5, 2026-03-26)

## Executive Summary

**NTN4 is a novel IPF GWAS locus identified by Chin et al. 2025** (rs7957346, 12q23.1, coloc PP=0.649 in lung). Unlike established mucin and telomere targets, NTN4 is an endothelial guidance cue with roles in vascular barrier integrity, angiogenesis, and inflammation.

**Challenge**: The genetic direction is counterintuitive:
- **GWAS risk allele** increases NTN4 expression
- **NTN4 biology**: Promotes endothelial barrier integrity, reduces inflammation, reduces senescence
- **Expected genetics**: Higher NTN4 should be protective, not harmful
- **Resolution**: Either (1) IPF-specific NTN4 mechanisms differ from endothelial paradigm, or (2) NTN4 plays dual roles depending on cellular context

---

## Part 1: NTN4 Gene and Protein Structure

### 1.1 Gene and Expression

**Gene**: NTN4 (Netrin-4), chromosome 12q23.1
**HGNC ID**: HGNC:7962
**Protein**: ~600 amino acids, secreted guidance cue
**Family**: Netrin family (related to NTN1, NTN3, NTN5/SLIT3)

### 1.2 Structural Domains

**Netrin fold**: LD (laminin-like) domains + EGF-like domains (common to guidance cues)
**Functional domains**:
- Neogenin-binding (cell surface receptor)
- UNC5-binding (co-receptor for inhibitory signaling)
- Integrin-binding (potentially, via laminin-like domains)
- VEGF-independent angiogenesis regulation

### 1.3 Cellular Expression and Tissue Distribution

**Tissues with high NTN4**:
- Endothelial cells (primary source in vasculature)
- Epithelial cells (lung, GI, renal tubules)
- Immune cells (dendritic cells, some T cells)
- Fibroblasts (context-dependent)

**IPF-relevant tissues**:
- Alveolar-capillary interface (endothelial, epithelial, pericytes)
- Lung fibroblasts (may have altered NTN4 signaling)
- Infiltrating immune cells

## Part 2: NTN4 Mechanisms in Vascular Biology (Established)

### 2.1 Endothelial Barrier Integrity — Pro-Barrier Functions

**Recent study (2024, Spandidos/PubMed):**
- NTN4 overexpression in HUVECs → increased VE-cadherin expression
- Mechanism: NF-κB signaling pathway activation
- Result: **Decreased endothelial permeability** (tighter barrier)

**VE-cadherin context:**
- VE-cadherin is the primary adherens junction protein in endothelium
- Loss of VE-cadherin → vascular leakiness, increased permeability to fluid and leukocytes
- NTN4 → ↑VE-cadherin → stronger barrier

**Functional outcomes:**
- NTN4 reduces endothelial migration (stabilizes junctions)
- Promotes endothelial cell survival
- Maintains vascular system formation and stability

### 2.2 Anti-Angiogenic Properties

**Paradoxical function**:
- NTN4 inhibits pathological angiogenesis (via neogenin + UNC5B co-stimulation)
- But is required for maintenance of mature blood vessels
- Balance: VEGF (pro-angiogenic) vs NTN4 (anti-angiogenic, stabilizing)

**In ocular disease**:
- NTN4 knockout → abnormal retinal neovascularization (pathological new vessel growth)
- NTN4 presence → normal vessel maturation and remodeling

**Mechanism**:
- Neogenin + UNC5B → intracellular Unc5b signaling → repulsion, stabilization
- vs. Neogenin alone (adhesion)

### 2.3 Anti-Inflammatory and Anti-Senescence in Endothelium

**Recent findings (2024-2025)**:
- Reduced NTN4 in endothelial cells → **increased vascular cell adhesion molecules** (VCAM-1, ICAM-1)
- Reduced NTN4 → impaired barrier function
- Reduced NTN4 → **increased endothelial senescence** markers
- NTN4 acts as **anti-inflammatory and anti-senescence factor** in endothelial cells

**Inflammatory context:**
- Low NTN4 → more VCAM-1/ICAM-1 → more leukocyte adhesion and transmigration
- High NTN4 → less leukocyte infiltration → reduced lung inflammation

## Part 3: NTN4 in Pathological Angiogenesis and Fibrosis

### 3.1 IPF Vascular Pathology: Abnormal Angiogenesis

**IPF vascular changes**:
1. **Capillary rarefaction** (loss of microvascular density)
2. **Abnormal angiogenesis** in areas of fibrosis (dysregulated new vessel formation)
3. **Endothelial-mesenchymal transition (EndMT)**: Some endothelial cells acquire myofibroblast markers
4. **Vascular leakage**: Compromised alveolar-capillary barrier

### 3.2 NTN4 in the Context of IPF Angiogenesis

**Two possible mechanisms explaining GWAS association**:

#### **Hypothesis A: NTN4 Promotes Excessive Angiogenesis (Risk)**
- In IPF, abnormal angiogenesis is pathological (creates fibrotic niche)
- Increased NTN4 (risk allele) → excessive pro-angiogenic signaling → pathological new vessels
- Supporting: NTN4 has pro-angiogenic roles (via neogenin + VEGF synergy)
- **Status**: Speculative; needs IPF-specific data

#### **Hypothesis B: NTN4 Overexpression Impairs Endothelial Function Locally (Indirect Harm)**
- In IPF lung fibroblast-endothelial coculture, high NTN4 might paradoxically reduce local endothelial signaling capacity
- Fibroblasts respond differently to NTN4 than epithelium (see Part 4)
- **Status**: Requires mechanistic study

#### **Hypothesis C: NTN4 Is a Marker of Dysregulated Endothelial-Fibroblast Crosstalk**
- GWAS signal for increased NTN4 may reflect abnormal endothelial-fibroblast signaling
- The risk allele may correlate with fibroblast-induced endothelial dysfunction
- **Status**: Requires fine-mapping and single-cell analysis

### 3.3 NTN4 and Endothelial-Mesenchymal Transition (EndMT)

**EndMT in IPF**:
- Endothelial cells with reduced barrier function may undergo EndMT → acquire α-SMA → become myofibroblasts
- Contributes to myofibroblast pool
- TGF-β is the primary driver

**NTN4 role (speculative)**:
- NTN4 promotes VE-cadherin → may protect against EndMT
- High NTN4 might reduce EndMT risk
- If so: risk allele increasing NTN4 should be **protective**, not harmful
- **Contradiction**: This contradicts GWAS direction

## Part 4: NTN4 in Fibroblasts and Non-Endothelial Contexts

### 4.1 NTN4 in Fibroblast-Endothelial Crosstalk

**Fibroblast signaling via netrin pathways**:
- Fibroblasts express neogenin and UNC5 receptors
- Can respond to endothelial NTN4 paracrine signaling
- Context: In osteoarthritis (recent 2025 study), fibroblastic cells exposed to NTN4 show pro-inflammatory cytokine production

**In IPF context**:
- Lung fibroblasts may aberrantly respond to high NTN4
- Speculative: High local NTN4 (from endothelium or epithelium) stimulates fibroblast IL-6, TNF-α, TGF-β production
- Creates a feedforward loop: endothelial stress → high NTN4 → fibroblast activation

### 4.2 NTN4 and ECM Remodeling

**Laminin-like domains of NTN4**:
- Can interact with integrin-binding epitopes
- May modulate fibroblast-ECM interactions
- Could affect fibroblast migration and ECM synthesis

**Status**: Speculative; ECM-NTN4 interactions not well-characterized in lung fibrosis

### 4.3 Recent Osteoarthritis Parallel (2025 Study)

**Finding**: NTN4 stimulates pro-inflammatory cytokine production in synovial fibroblasts
- NTN4 in synovial fluid from OA patients correlates with inflammation markers
- OA is a different inflammatory milieu than IPF
- But suggests NTN4 may have cell-type-specific, context-dependent effects

## Part 5: Resolving the NTN4 Paradox — Genetic Direction

### 5.1 Statement of the Paradox

| Property | Evidence | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| GWAS risk allele | rs7957346, increases NTN4 expression | ↑ NTN4 = risk |
| Endothelial function | NTN4 → barrier integrity, anti-inflammation | ↑ NTN4 = protective |
| Angiogenesis | NTN4 inhibits pathological angiogenesis | ↑ NTN4 = protective |
| Senescence | NTN4 = anti-senescence in endothelium | ↑ NTN4 = protective |
| Reconciliation | ??? | ??? |

### 5.2 Possible Resolutions

#### **Resolution 1: IPF-Specific NTN4 Mechanism Differs from Endothelial Paradigm**
- In epithelial or fibroblast context, high NTN4 may promote pathological signaling
- Endothelium-centric studies don't capture IPF lung cell biology
- **Action**: Search for fibroblast-specific NTN4 studies; seek single-cell RNA-seq of IPF lungs with NTN4 expression by cell type

#### **Resolution 2: Fine-Mapping Issue — Risk Allele Is Not NTN4, but Linked Variant**
- rs7957346 may not be causal for NTN4 expression
- True causal variant could affect nearby gene (SLC6A6 at 3p25? No, wrong chromosome)
- Or: rs7957346 affects distant enhancer for different gene
- **Action**: Review Chin 2025 paper for coloc evidence (coloc PP=0.649 is suggestive but <0.8 threshold)

#### **Resolution 3: Genetic Effect Is Reduced NTN4 Risk, Not Increased**
- Recheck allele frequency and direction of association in Chin et al. 2025
- If effect allele is protective (OR<1), but we're reading it as risk, this resolves paradox
- **Action**: Check Chin 2025 paper primary table

#### **Resolution 4: NTN4 Promotes Aberrant Angiogenesis in IPF-Specific Conditions**
- Standard endothelial biology shows NTN4 inhibits pathological angiogenesis
- But in IPF-inflamed, fibrotic lung with high TGF-β, may flip to pro-angiogenic
- **Action**: Requires experimental work (not feasible computationally)

### 5.3 Most Likely Explanation (Conditional)

**Based on literature, most plausible path**:
1. Chin 2025 correctly identified NTN4 as GWAS signal
2. Risk allele increases NTN4 expression (as stated in their abstract)
3. **In IPF lungs specifically** (high inflammation, TGF-β, endothelial stress), elevated NTN4 may contribute to pathological remodeling
4. Endothelial cells under chronic inflammation may revert from barrier-promoting to angiogenic or senescent state despite NTN4
5. Fibroblast response to NTN4 dominates over endothelial protection in IPF context

**Alternative**: The coloc PP=0.649 is borderline, and true causal variant is nearby non-coding element affecting a different gene.

## Part 6: Therapeutic Implications for NTN4

### 6.1 Current Druggability Status

**NTN4 as target**:
- Secreted protein → accessible to antibodies, peptides
- No known small-molecule ligands
- No approved drugs
- Pharos classification: Tbio (target biology known)

**Potential modalities**:
1. **Anti-NTN4 antibody**: Block NTN4 signaling (would reduce downstream endothelial barrier protection — likely wrong direction)
2. **Pro-NTN4 activation**: Increase NTN4 function (aligns with protective endothelial phenotype — but contradicts GWAS)
3. **Neogenin/UNC5 modulation**: Block or enhance NTN4 receptor signaling
4. **Gene therapy**: Increase NTN4 in endothelial cells specifically (not feasible for IPF)

### 6.2 Trial Design Considerations If Resolving Paradox

**If high NTN4 is truly harmful in IPF**:
- Anti-NTN4 antibody trial in IPF patients with elevated plasma NTN4
- Requires development of new therapeutic

**If high NTN4 is actually protective (paradox is our error)**:
- Reconsider genetic association; may be non-causal variant
- Or: focus on restoring endothelial barrier via NTN4 support

**If paradox unresolved**:
- Classify as Tier 3 Suggestive
- Deprioritize vs. Tier 1-2 targets with clear mechanism
- Monitor for functional studies from IPF community

### 6.3 Updated Ranking Recommendation

**Current ranking**: Rank 21, Tier 2 Moderate, Direction = "Unknown"
- Correct, given mechanistic ambiguity
- Novelty score remains "Very High" (new locus, new biology)
- Druggability Tier 3 (no known ligands) — appropriate

**Consider downgrading if**:
- Chin 2025 fine-mapping shows signal is non-NTN4
- Or if IPF functional studies show NTN4 not expressed in relevant cell type

- **No clinical trial data** with anti-NTN4 or pro-NTN4 agents in any lung disease
- **No pharmacokinetics** for potential anti-NTN4 biologics
