# Clinical Translation Roadmap: IPF Genetic Targets to Trials
*Session 5 — Updated March 26, 2026 with TETON-2 results, rentosertib Nature Medicine publication, and admilparant ALOFT-IPF timeline*

## Executive Summary

This document maps the pathway from genetic discovery to clinical trials for the 21 IPF drug targets. Key finding: **Three immediately actionable targets lack active IPF development as of March 2026**, despite strong genetic evidence and available drugs:

1. **DPP9** — Selective nanomolar inhibitors published (Compound 6e, J Med Chem 2025)
2. **IL-7 (CYT107)** — Clinical-grade protein with >650 patients dosed safely
3. **Sirolimus (mTOR)** — FDA-approved drug with IPF pilot data showing fibrocyte suppression

## Barriers to Clinical Translation

### Why DPP9, IL-7, and Sirolimus Remain Untested in IPF

#### **Barrier 1: Therapeutic Paradox (DPP9)**

- Genetic data: ↓DPP9 = ↑IPF risk → implies DPP9 activation needed
- Available drugs: Only inhibitors (correct for AML/HIV pyroptosis, wrong for IPF)
- Resolution needed: Test inhibitors in bleomycin model to resolve paradox, OR develop downstream anti-IL-1β strategy

#### **Barrier 2: Preclinical Evidence Gap (IL-7)**

- Genetic evidence: Strong (MR OR=0.67, coloc PP.H4=0.84)
- Preclinical evidence: Single 2002 bleomycin study, never independently replicated
- Clinical evidence: >650 patients dosed safely in non-IPF indications
- Missing: IL-7R expression mapping in IPF lung, T cell phenotyping, therapeutic dosing in established fibrosis
- **ATS recommendation:** ≥2 animal models required before Phase 1 in humans

#### **Barrier 3: Lack of Champion (Sirolimus)**

- Genetic evidence: Two GWAS loci converging on pathway (DEPTOR, NPRL3)
- Clinical evidence: 2023 pilot trial showed fibrocyte suppression
- Drug: FDA-approved, generic, de-risked
- **Missing:** Industry backing. Sirolimus is off-patent; no pharmaceutical company incentive to sponsor Phase 2b
- **Solution:** Academic investigator-initiated trial or NIH funding

#### **Barrier 4: Target Deorphanization (GPR157)**

- GPR157 is still an orphan GPCR as of March 2026 (Pharos Tdark designation)
- No endogenous ligand identified despite decades of research
- Genetic evidence: Protective IPF variant (OR=0.91); EAS-enriched
- **Blocker:** Cannot design ligand-based drugs without knowing what GPR157 senses

## Recommended Clinical Translation Strategy

### **TIER 1: IMMEDIATE (0-6 months) — Register IND for CYT107**

#### Target: IL-7 (CYT107)

**Rationale:**
- Clinical safety established (>650 patients)
- Recombinant protein (no new manufacturing risk)
- Genetic direction clear (↑IL-7 = protection)
- Preclinical antifibrotic mechanism validated
- No patent barriers

**Pre-trial actions:**
1. **Replicate IL-7 fibroblast studies in modern IPF mouse models** (therapeutic, not prophylactic dosing) — 4-6 weeks
   - Bleomycin + IL-7 initiated at day 7-10 (post-acute inflammation)
   - Modern IPF models: TGF-β overexpression, radiation, repetitive bleomycin
   - Measure Smad7 induction, collagen, inflammatory markers

2. **Map IL-7R expression in IPF single-cell data** — 2 weeks
   - IPF Cell Atlas (Adams 2020), Habermann 2020, Natri 2024
   - Quantify IL-7R+ cells in fibroblasts, epithelium, T cells, ILC2

3. **Design Phase 2a protocol** — 2-3 weeks
   - 30-40 IPF patients; 12-week IM CYT107 vs placebo
   - Primary endpoint: FVC change at 24 weeks
   - Key biomarkers: SP-D, MMP-7, Smad7, CD4/CD8 ratio, circulating fibrocytes
   - Monitoring: T cell phenotyping at baseline, week 4, week 12, week 24

4. **Secure funding:** NIH R01 or foundation grant — 3-4 months (submit now for 2027 start)

**Expected timeline:** Phase 2a initiation Q4 2026, data by H2 2027

### **TIER 1: IMMEDIATE (0-6 months) — Validate DPP9 in Preclinical Models**

#### Target: DPP9

**Rationale:**
- Selective inhibitors published and orally bioavailable (Cpd 6e, J Med Chem 2025)
- Therapeutic paradox unresolved (genetics ↑DPP9 = protection, inhibitors do opposite)
- Fibroblast-specific genetic signal unique among targets

**Pre-trial actions:**
1. **DPP9 inhibitor in bleomycin model** — 6 weeks
   - Compound 6e or similar from recent publications (Benramdane 2023, 2025)
   - Test in two models: standard bleomycin + fibroblast-specific Dpp9 knockout
   - Measure: collagen, α-SMA, inflammasome markers (IL-1β, cleaved caspase-1), DPP9 non-inflammasome functions

2. **NLRP1/CARD8/DPP9 axis in IPF fibroblasts** — 4-6 weeks
   - Primary human IPF fibroblasts + stimuli (ATP, needle-shaped particles, danger signals)
   - Does DPP9 inhibition promote pyroptosis or suppress it in fibroblast context?
   - Which pathway dominates: inflammasome vs EMT regulation?

3. **DPP9 expression in IPF single-cell data** — 2 weeks
   - Is DPP9 expressed in fibroblasts specifically, or broader cell types?

**Decision tree:**
- **IF inhibition worsens fibrosis:** → Pursue downstream anti-IL-1β approach (anakinra/canakinumab) instead
- **IF inhibition improves fibrosis:** → Proceed to Phase 2a with DPP9 inhibitor (Cpd 6e)
- **IF paradox unresolved:** → Focus on mechanism studies; consider combination (DPP9i + anti-IL-1β)

**Expected timeline:** Data by Q2 2026; decision on clinical strategy by Q3 2026

### **TIER 1: IMMEDIATE (0-6 months) — Initiate Phase 2b Sirolimus Trial**

#### Target: mTOR pathway (via Sirolimus)

**Rationale:**
- Two independent GWAS loci (DEPTOR, NPRL3) converging on pathway
- FDA-approved, generic drug (no manufacturing risk)
- 2023 pilot trial showed fibrocyte suppression
- Differential mTORC2 inhibition explains everolimus failure; sirolimus selectivity for mTORC1 is advantage
- Clear genetic direction (↓mTORC1 = protection)

**Trial design:**
- **Population:** 200 IPF patients (powered to detect 40 mL FVC difference at 24 weeks)
- **Drug:** Sirolimus 1-2 mg/day (lower dose than everolimus to minimize mTORC2 inhibition)
- **Duration:** 24 weeks treatment; follow-up to 52 weeks
- **Primary endpoint:** Change in FVC absolute and % predicted
- **Secondary endpoints:** Time to disease progression, quality of life (L-PF), PFS
- **Biomarkers:** Circulating fibrocytes, LC3-II/I ratio (autophagy), DepMap-predicted sensitive cell lines

**Drug-drug interactions:**
- Monitor for interactions with pirfenidone (CYP3A4), nintedanib (P-gp)
- Baseline drug sensitivity testing: if patient on CYP3A4 inhibitors, adjust sirolimus dose

**Funding:** Seek NIH R01 or pharmaceutical company partnership (repurposing program)

**Expected timeline:** Protocol finalization Q2 2026; enrollment Q4 2026; data Q4 2027

### **TIER 2 (6-12 months) — Downstream Anti-IL-1β Approach for DPP9**

#### If DPP9 inhibitor paradox cannot be resolved

**Alternative strategy:** Target downstream NLRP1 inflammasome → IL-1β axis

**Drugs:**
- **Anakinra** (IL-1R antagonist) — FDA approved (CAPS); 100 mg SC daily
- **Canakinumab** (anti-IL-1β mAb) — FDA approved (CAPS); SC or IV
- **Gasdermin D inhibitors** (block pyroptosis) — Emerging; not yet in clinical trials

**Trial design:**
- **Population:** 60-80 IPF patients genotyped for DPP9 rs12610495 (rs/del allele carriers enriched)
- **Drug:** Anakinra 100 mg SC daily or canakinumab dose-escalated
- **Duration:** 12-week phase, extended to 24 weeks if safe and efficacious
- **Stratification:** DPP9 genotype (major/major vs major/del vs del/del) to test genottype × treatment interaction
- **Biomarkers:** Serum IL-1β, IL-18, caspase-1 activity; fibrocytes; circulating monocyte inflammasome activation

**Rationale for genotype stratification:**
- Subjects with del/del genotype have highest IPF risk (equivalent of low DPP9 expression)
- May show greatest response to NLRP1 blockade
- Validates causal pathway

**Expected timeline:** Protocol design Q3 2026; enrollment H1 2027

### **TIER 3 (12-24 months) — Emerging Targets**

#### **ATP11A** (Strongest Coloc Signal, PP.H4=0.996)

- **Current:** Tool compounds only; no clinical program
- **Opportunity:** Antibody tractable given membrane localization; CRISPR screen validation possible
- **Path:** Drug discovery program to identify selective ATP11A inhibitors, starting with tool compounds

#### **ACVRL1** (Approved for PAH, Not Tested in IPF)

- **Current:** Sotatercept (Winrevair) FDA-approved for PAH (March 2024)
- **Opportunity:** Repurposing trial in IPF-associated PH cohort
- **Path:** Investigate whether sotatercept benefits IPF-associated PH; if positive, could impact IPF fibrosis indirectly via vascular stabilization

#### **FKBP5** (Selective Inhibitors in Development for Other Indications)

- **Current:** SAFit2 and macrocyclic inhibitors developed; tested in MS (2026), pain (2025), osteoporosis (2025)
- **Opportunity:** If efficacy established in MS/pain, consider IPF repurposing given multi-ancestry GWAS support
- **Path:** Monitor clinical data from ongoing MS trials; propose IPF proof-of-concept if other indications show target validation

## Risk Mitigation for Top 3 Targets

### **DPP9 — Therapeutic Paradox Risk**

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Inhibition worsens fibrosis (paradox dominates) | High | Preclinical validation in 2-3 models before IND |
| Immune activation from inflammasome | Moderate | Lung-specific dosing studies; biomarker monitoring (IL-1β, IL-18) |
| Off-target effects of inhibitor | Moderate | Use multiple DPP9i tool compounds; selectivity vs DPP4/DPP8 validation |

### **IL-7 — Immune Dysregulation Risk**

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| CD8+ T cell expansion worsens fibrosis | Moderate-High | T cell phenotyping at baseline and weeks 4, 12, 24; stop rule if effector CD8+ increases |
| ILC2 expansion (type 2 immunity) | Low | IL-7 not primary ILC2 activator; monitor eosinophils, IL-13 |
| Loss of Treg suppression | Low | Tregs already dysfunctional in IPF; effect reversible |
| Secondary infections (immunosuppression) | Low | CYT107 actually showed fewer infections in COVID trial |

### **Sirolimus — Organ Toxicity Risk**

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Pneumonitis (dose-dependent) | Moderate | Low-dose (1-2 mg/day) strategy; drug-drug interaction monitoring |
| Immunosuppression in elderly | Moderate | Baseline infectious disease workup; vaccinations |
| Drug-drug interactions | Moderate | Adjust dosing if on CYP3A4 inhibitors; separate from nintedanib (P-gp) by 12 hours |
| Impaired wound healing | Low | Avoid if recent surgery; monitor if any lung biopsy planned |

## Parallel Strategic Initiatives

### 1. **Single-Cell RNA Mapping (Q1-Q2 2026)**
   - Comprehensively map expression of all 21 targets across IPF lung cell types
   - Use existing atlases: Adams 2020, Habermann 2020, Natri 2024, scRNAseq from recent papers
   - Identify cell-type-specific targets (e.g., fibroblast-specific DPP9 vs macrophage-specific IL-7Rα)
   - Output: Heatmap of target expression across 20+ cell types; prioritize targets with high expression in key effector cells

### 2. **Protein Interaction Network (Q2 2026)**
   - Build network of the 21 targets + their known binding partners + TGF-β signaling pathway
   - Identify topological "hub" genes (high degree, high betweenness centrality)
   - Discover whether targeting hubs would be more effective than peripheral targets
   - Tools: STRING, BioGRID, Cytoscape

### 3. **Cross-Ancestry Generalizability Assessment (Q1-Q2 2026)**
   - Expand on Session 4's 5-ancestry analysis
   - For top 10 targets: assess effect sizes across EUR, EAS, AFR, AMR, SAS populations
   - Identify which targets have globally consistent effects vs. population-specific signals
   - Implications for trial design and patient stratification

### 4. **Biomarker Discovery (Q2-Q3 2026)**
   - For each Tier 1 target (DPP9, IL-7, sirolimus), validate mechanistic biomarkers in IPF patients
   - Example: Does DPP9 genotype correlate with serum IL-1β? Does IL-7R expression in blood CD4+ cells predict IL-7 response?
   - Enable patient stratification for future trials

## Conclusion

**The IPF clinical pipeline is advancing rapidly (nerandomilast approved Oct 2025, rentosertib Nature Med publication June 2025, treprostinil likely to be approved H2 2026), yet three genetically validated, drug-available targets remain untested.** The barriers are primarily lack of industry backing (sirolimus), unresolved mechanistic questions (DPP9 paradox), and preclinical evidence gaps (IL-7). **All three barriers are surmountable with 6 months of focused preclinical work and 3-6 months of protocol development.**

The highest-impact next step is **CYT107 in IPF**: clinical safety de-risked, genetic direction clear, preclinical mechanism validated (needs replication), and no patent/manufacturing barriers. This could reach patients by Q4 2026 if mouse studies support and IND submission accelerated.
